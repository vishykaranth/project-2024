# Kafka ACKS — Principal Engineer Cheat Sheet

## What is `acks`?

`acks` controls **how many brokers must confirm a write** before the producer considers the message successfully sent.

In short: **latency vs durability**.

---

## `acks=0` — Fire and Forget

### What happens

* Producer does **not wait** for any broker response
* Message may be lost without the producer knowing

### Use cases

* Metrics, logs, clickstreams where **loss is acceptable**
* Ultra-low latency > correctness

### Risks / Anti-patterns

❌ Silent data loss
❌ No retries possible
❌ Not acceptable for business data

### Interview one-liner

> “`acks=0` optimizes latency by explicitly accepting data loss.”

---

## `acks=1` — Leader Acknowledgement (Default)

### What happens

* Leader writes to its local log
* Does **not wait** for replicas

### Use cases

* High throughput systems
* Some durability, but tolerates rare loss

### Failure scenario

* Leader crashes **before replicas replicate**
  → message lost

### Risks / Anti-patterns

❌ Often mistaken as “safe”
❌ Not enough for financial or critical events

### Interview one-liner

> “`acks=1` protects against broker availability, not data loss.”

---

## `acks=all` (or `-1`) — Strongest Durability

### What happens

* Leader waits for **all in-sync replicas (ISR)**
* Message committed only after quorum

### Use cases

* Payments, inventory, orders, user actions
* Exactly-once pipelines (with idempotent producer)

### Trade-offs

* Higher latency
* Lower throughput

### Required for correctness

* `acks=all`
* `min.insync.replicas ≥ 2`
* Replication factor ≥ 3

### Interview one-liner

> “`acks=all` trades latency for durability and is mandatory for critical data.”

---

## The Hidden Gotcha: `acks=all` Alone Is NOT Enough

### ❗ Common Mistake

Using `acks=all` with:

```
min.insync.replicas = 1
```

### Why this is bad

* Kafka will ACK with **only the leader alive**
* Still lose data on leader failure

### Correct setup

```
replication.factor = 3
min.insync.replicas = 2
acks = all
```

### Interview power line

> “`acks=all` without `min.insync.replicas` is a false sense of safety.”

---

## ACKS During DB Outage (Spike Scenario)

### Correct behavior

* Producers keep writing to Kafka (`acks=all`)
* Consumers fail → lag increases
* Data is safely buffered

### Why ACKS matter here

* Ensures data isn’t lost during outages
* Allows replay after DB recovery

### Interview tie-in

> “ACKS define whether Kafka is a buffer or a liability during failures.”

---

## ACKS vs Throughput vs Latency

| ACKS | Latency    | Throughput | Durability |
| ---- | ---------- | ---------- | ---------- |
| 0    | 🔥 Lowest  | 🔥 Highest | ❌ None     |
| 1    | ⚖️ Medium  | ⚖️ High    | ⚠️ Partial |
| all  | 🐢 Highest | 🐢 Lower   | ✅ Strong   |

> For telemetry, I explicitly accept loss with `acks=0`.”
