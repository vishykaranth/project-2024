# 🧠 Principal Engineer Scalability Cheat Sheet

---

## 1️⃣ Architecture: Right Tool, Right Time

### ✅ Strong Answer

* Start with **modular monolith**
* Scale **vertically first**, then horizontally
* Introduce **microservices only when team boundaries or failure isolation demand it**

### 🚫 Anti-Pattern

❌ “Microservices scale better by default”
❌ “Shard early to be safe”

### 🎯 Interview Line

> “I delay distributed systems until simplicity becomes the bottleneck.”

---

## 2️⃣ Horizontal Scaling (Compute)

### ✅ Strong Answer

* Scale stateless services horizontally
* Validate **downstream scalability first** (DB, cache, queues)
* Use **autoscaling with safeguards**

### 🚫 Anti-Pattern

❌ “CPU is low, just add instances”
❌ Ignoring DB / cache limits

### 🎯 Interview Line

> “Horizontal scaling only works if dependencies scale proportionally.”

---

## 3️⃣ Database Bottlenecks (Reads)

### ✅ Strong Answer

* Read replicas
* Query optimization
* Caching with **stale-while-revalidate**
* Request coalescing

### 🚫 Anti-Pattern

❌ “Increase TTLs”
❌ Cache as source of truth

### 🎯 Interview Line

> “Caches are performance optimizations, not consistency mechanisms.”

---

## 4️⃣ Cache Stampede / Thundering Herd

### ✅ Strong Answer

* Serve **stale data**
* Singleflight / request coalescing
* TTL jitter
* Pre-warming for known spikes

### 🚫 Anti-Pattern

❌ Blocking users on cache miss
❌ Letting all requests hit DB

### 🎯 Interview Line

> “I trade bounded staleness for system survival.”

---

## 5️⃣ Write-Heavy Spikes (Likes, Counters, Inventory)

### ✅ Strong Answer

* Async writes via Kafka / queues
* Write-behind caching (Redis)
* Batch updates
* Sharding by high-cardinality keys

### 🚫 Anti-Pattern

❌ Synchronous writes everywhere
❌ Per-request DB updates

### 🎯 Interview Line

> “The key is decoupling user latency from durable writes.”

---

## 6️⃣ Kafka as a Shock Absorber

### ✅ Strong Answer

* Kafka buffers spikes
* Producers continue during DB outage
* Consumers lag safely
* Replay after recovery

### 🚫 Anti-Pattern

❌ “Kafka absorbs infinite traffic”
❌ No disk / lag monitoring

### 🎯 Interview Line

> “Kafka buys time — it doesn’t eliminate backpressure.”

---

## 7️⃣ Consistency & Correctness

### ✅ Strong Answer

* Strong consistency for money, inventory, auth
* Eventual consistency only where business allows
* Idempotency everywhere

### 🚫 Anti-Pattern

❌ “Eventual consistency is fine”
❌ “Kafka guarantees exactly-once”

### 🎯 Interview Line

> “Consistency is a business decision, not a technical preference.”

---

## 8️⃣ Sharding & Hot Keys

### ✅ Strong Answer

* Shard only after vertical scaling fails
* Detect hot keys via metrics
* Rate-limit or isolate hotspots

### 🚫 Anti-Pattern

❌ Uniform hashing solves hotspots
❌ No rebalancing plan

### 🎯 Interview Line

> “Sharding solves throughput, not contention.”

---

## 9️⃣ Resilience & Failure Handling

### ✅ Strong Answer

* Circuit breakers
* Bounded retries + jitter
* Graceful degradation
* Load shedding

### 🚫 Anti-Pattern

❌ Retry until success
❌ Let everything timeout

### 🎯 Interview Line

> “Fail fast to protect the system, not the request.”

---

## 🔟 Observability & Metrics

### ✅ Strong Answer

* Monitor P95 / P99
* Track consumer lag
* Cost per request
* Saturation metrics

### 🚫 Anti-Pattern

❌ Average latency
❌ “We’ll add monitoring later”

### 🎯 Interview Line

> “If you can’t see it, you can’t scale it.”

---

## 1️⃣1️⃣ Cost & Sustainability

### ✅ Strong Answer

* Reduce cost per request at scale
* Avoid premature infra spend
* Optimize hot paths only

### 🚫 Anti-Pattern

❌ Performance without cost awareness

### 🎯 Interview Line

> “True scalability lowers marginal cost.”

---

## 1️⃣2️⃣ People & Org Scaling (Often Missed)

### ✅ Strong Answer

* Simple systems
* Clear ownership
* Low bus factor
* Oncall-friendly design

### 🚫 Anti-Pattern

❌ “Only experts can touch this”
❌ Architecture purity

### 🎯 Interview Line

> “A scalable system is one teams can safely operate.”

---

## 🚨 Interview Red Flags (Instant Down-Level)

* “Best practice says…”
* “Always / Never”
* Over-engineering early
* Ignoring failure scenarios
* Ignoring cost or ops

---

## 🏁 Bar-Raiser Closing Statement (Use This)

> “I optimize for simplicity first, scalability second, and correctness always — because systems fail at their edges, not their averages.”
