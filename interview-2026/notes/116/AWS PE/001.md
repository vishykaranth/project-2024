# What “Principal on AWS” really means
At this level they are NOT testing: “Do you know what S3 is?”

They ARE testing:
- Can you design cloud platforms
- Can you operate at 10× scale
- Can you make org-wide technical decisions
- Can you balance cost, resilience, and speed

You are expected to think in:

* multi-account strategy
* multi-region architecture
* blast-radius isolation
* operability
* cost as a first-class metric

---

# 🧠 2️⃣ Core Interview Pillars

## 1. Cloud Architecture & Trade-offs

You’ll be asked:

> “Design a globally scalable service on AWS.”

They expect:

* Why ALB vs NLB vs API Gateway?
* Why ECS vs EKS vs Lambda?
* Why Aurora vs DynamoDB?
* How do you isolate tenants?

---

## 2. Resilience & Availability (Very heavily weighted)

You must naturally talk in:

* cell-based architecture
* bulkheads
* graceful degradation
* failover strategy
* regional evacuation

---

## 3. Cost Engineering (Principal signal)

Not “we scale”

But:

> “This design reduces cost by 38% because…”

They want:

* right-sizing
* spot strategy
* storage tiering
* compute vs cache trade-off

---

## 4. Operability

You’ll be probed on:

* SLOs
* P99
* observability
* automated remediation
* runbooks

---

## 5. Data strategy

You must reason about:

* consistency vs latency
* partition strategy
* rebalancing
* multi-region replication

---

# 🏗️ 3️⃣ Canonical System Design Question

## 🔥 “Design a multi-tenant SaaS platform on AWS”

Your answer should flow like:

### 1. Requirements

Functional + non-functional:

* tenant isolation model
* scale target
* availability target
* compliance

---

### 2. Cell-based architecture (Principal signal)

```
Route53
   ↓
Regional cell
   ↓
Tenant shard
```

Why?

👉 Limits blast radius
👉 Enables independent scaling

---

### 3. Compute layer decision

Trade-offs:

| Service | When                          |
| ------- | ----------------------------- |
| EKS     | platform control, portability |
| ECS     | simpler ops                   |
| Lambda  | spiky workloads               |

---

### 4. Data layer

You must explain:

* hot partition handling
* read/write scaling
* multi-region strategy

---

### 5. Async backbone

Event-driven:

* SQS / SNS / Kafka (MSK)

Why:

* decoupling
* replay
* backpressure

---

### 6. Resilience patterns

* retries with jitter
* circuit breakers
* hedging
* fallback paths

---

### 7. Operability

* RED / USE metrics
* distributed tracing
* SLO-driven alerts

---

### 8. Cost optimization

* Spot for stateless
* Graviton
* storage lifecycle
* cache hit ratio targets

---

# ☁️ 4️⃣ Deep-Dive Topics You WILL Be Pushed On

## Multi-region strategy

Active-active vs active-passive

You must discuss:

* data conflict resolution
* routing policy
* RPO / RTO

---

## Networking (huge differentiator)

Most candidates fail here.

You should be fluent in:

* VPC design
* subnet tiering
* PrivateLink
* cross-account access
* egress cost control

---

## IAM & security model

Principal-level expectations:

* least privilege at scale
* permission boundaries
* org SCP strategy
* workload identity

---

# 📊 5️⃣ Metrics You Should Naturally Use

They expect numbers:

* P99 latency
* MTTR
* error budget
* cost per tenant
* cost per request

---

# 🧪 6️⃣ Operational Scenario Round

You’ll get:

> “Latency increased 4× in one region — what do you do?”

They expect:

1. reduce blast radius
2. shift traffic
3. degrade gracefully
4. root-cause using telemetry

---

# 🏆 7️⃣ Org-Level Principal Signals

You must show:

* influencing without authority
* setting technical standards
* platform thinking
* long-term roadmap
