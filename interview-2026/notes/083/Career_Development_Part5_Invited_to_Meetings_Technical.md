# You're Invited to Meetings Because You're Technical (Here's How to Stop It)

## Overview

This video addresses why technical developers are often pulled into excessive meetings and provides strategies to reduce meeting invitations while maintaining your technical contribution and career growth.

## Strategies to Reduce Meetings

### Strategy 1: The "Async First" Principle

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│         Async First Approach                          │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

Instead of Meeting:
├─ Share written document
├─ Request feedback via comments
├─ Schedule meeting only if needed
└─ Default to async communication

Benefits:
├─ Saves time for everyone
├─ Better documentation
├─ More thoughtful responses
└─ Reduces meeting load
```

### Strategy 2: The "Office Hours" Model

Instead of ad-hoc meetings:
- Set specific times for technical consultation
- Direct people to your office hours
- Reduce random meeting invitations

**Example:**
"I'm available for technical questions Tuesdays and Thursdays, 2-3 PM. For urgent issues, please Slack me, and I'll respond within 2 hours."

### Strategy 3: The "Delegate or Document" Rule

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│         Delegate or Document Framework                 │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

When Invited to Meeting:
├─ Can someone else represent? → Delegate
├─ Can this be a document? → Create doc
├─ Do I need to be there? → Evaluate
└─ Can I contribute async? → Request summary
```

### Strategy 4: The "Value Question"

Before accepting, ask:
- **What's my role?** - Am I decision-maker or just observer?
- **What's the outcome?** - What will be decided?
- **Can I contribute async?** - Can I review materials instead?
- **Is this recurring?** - Can I attend every other one?

## Practical Responses

### Response Templates

#### Template 1: Async Alternative
**Invitation**: "Can you join this meeting to discuss the API design?"

**Response**: "I'd prefer to review the design doc first and provide written feedback. If there are specific questions after that, we can schedule a focused 15-minute discussion."

#### Template 2: Delegate
**Invitation**: "We need technical input in this planning meeting."

**Response**: "[Team member] has been working on this area and can provide the technical perspective. I'm happy to review the decisions afterward."

#### Template 3: Office Hours
**Invitation**: "Can you join to answer technical questions?"

**Response**: "I'm in deep work mode right now. I'm available for technical questions during my office hours (Tuesdays 2-3 PM), or you can Slack me and I'll respond within 2 hours."

#### Template 4: Selective Attendance
**Invitation**: "Weekly status meeting - we'd like your input."

**Response**: "I can attend every other week. For the weeks I'm not there, I'll provide a written update, and [colleague] can represent our team."

## Creating Boundaries

### Setting Clear Expectations

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│         Meeting Boundaries Framework                   │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

Define Your Availability:
├─ Core hours for meetings: 10 AM - 12 PM
├─ Office hours for questions: Tues/Thurs 2-3 PM
├─ Deep work blocks: Mornings, Friday afternoons
└─ Emergency only: Outside these times

Communicate Clearly:
├─ Share your calendar preferences
├─ Set expectations in team meetings
├─ Update your Slack status
└─ Be consistent
```

### Calendar Management

1. **Block deep work time** - Mark as "busy" or "focus time"
2. **Set meeting-free days** - Protect certain days
3. **Limit meeting duration** - Default to 25 or 45 minutes
4. **Buffer time** - Add 15-minute buffers between meetings

## Best Practices

### Reducing Meeting Load
- **Ask for agenda** - evaluate if you need to attend
- **Request async alternative** - documents, reviews, office hours
- **Delegate strategically** - share responsibilities with team
- **Protect deep work** - block time for actual development
- **Create documentation** - reduce need for explanatory meetings

### When You Must Attend
- **Arrive prepared** - review materials beforehand
- **Be time-conscious** - keep discussions focused
- **Contribute meaningfully** - don't just observe
- **Follow up async** - send summary and action items

## Summary

Being invited to meetings because you're technical can consume your time and prevent you from doing actual development work. Reduce meeting overload by:
- Setting clear boundaries and availability
- Defaulting to async communication (documents, reviews)
- Creating office hours for scheduled consultation
- Delegating meeting attendance when appropriate
- Building self-service documentation and resources

Protect your time for deep work and actual development while still contributing strategically to important decisions.
