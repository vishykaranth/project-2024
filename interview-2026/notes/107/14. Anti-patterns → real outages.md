# 🚨 Scalability Anti-Patterns → Real Outages

---

## 1️⃣ Centralized ID Generator → Global Write Outage

### Anti-pattern

Single DB auto-increment / ID service for all writes.

### What happened

* ID service latency spiked
* All writes blocked
* Queues backed up
* Retries amplified load
* Full write outage

### Real-world examples

* Twitter (pre-Snowflake)
* Early Instagram
* Many fintech systems during traffic spikes

### Lesson

> **Uniqueness via coordination caps throughput.**

---

## 2️⃣ Hot Kafka Partition → Systemwide Backlog

### Anti-pattern

Partition key chosen without skew analysis.

### What happened

* One partition lagged minutes → hours
* Consumer group “healthy” overall
* Downstream SLAs missed
* Adding consumers didn’t help

### Seen at

* LinkedIn (early tracking pipelines)
* Uber event ingestion
* Ad-tech platforms

### Lesson

> **Kafka scalability is per-partition, not per-cluster.**

---

## 3️⃣ Cache Stampede → Database Meltdown

### Anti-pattern

Same TTL for popular keys.

### What happened

* Cache expired
* Thousands of concurrent cache misses
* DB overloaded
* Latency exploded
* Cascading failures

### Seen at

* AWS DynamoDB customers
* Large e-commerce flash sales
* Ticketing platforms

### Lesson

> **Caches fail *loudly* when they fail together.**

---

## 4️⃣ No Timeouts → Thread Pool Exhaustion

### Anti-pattern

Blocking calls without deadlines.

### What happened

* One slow dependency
* Threads pile up waiting
* Service stops responding
* Health checks still pass
* Full outage

### Famous example

* Netflix Hystrix origin story
* Java servlet-based systems everywhere

### Lesson

> **Latency without limits becomes failure.**

---

## 5️⃣ Retry Storm → Self-Inflicted DDoS

### Anti-pattern

Retries without backoff or budget.

### What happened

* Partial outage
* Clients retry immediately
* Traffic multiplies
* Dependency collapses completely

### Seen at

* AWS S3 incidents
* Payment gateways
* Internal microservice meshes

### Lesson

> **Retries are load multipliers.**

---

## 6️⃣ Leader-Only Writes → Throughput Ceiling

### Anti-pattern

All writes go through a single leader.

### What happened

* Leader CPU saturated
* Replicas idle
* Latency increases linearly with traffic
* Failover painful and slow

### Seen at

* Zookeeper-backed systems
* Early Elasticsearch clusters
* Master-heavy DB designs

### Lesson

> **Leadership is a scalability liability.**

---

## 7️⃣ Global Lock → Convoy Effect

### Anti-pattern

One lock protecting “rare” critical section.

### What happened

* Rare operation slowed
* All fast operations blocked
* Latency spikes everywhere
* Hard to reproduce locally

### Seen at

* JVM synchronized blocks
* Metadata services
* Config management systems

### Lesson

> **One slow operation can stall thousands.**

---

## 8️⃣ Strong Consistency Everywhere → Latency Explosion

### Anti-pattern

Linearizable reads/writes for non-critical data.

### What happened

* Cross-region coordination
* Tail latency skyrockets
* Availability drops
* System “works” but users leave

### Seen at

* Multi-region databases
* Global config services

### Lesson

> **Correctness beyond necessity is a tax.**

---

## 9️⃣ Monolithic Database → Deployment Outage

### Anti-pattern

One DB for unrelated workloads.

### What happened

* One heavy migration
* Locks critical tables
* All services impacted
* Rollback impossible

### Seen at

* SaaS platforms during schema changes
* Legacy ERP systems

### Lesson

> **Shared state couples failures.**

---

## 🔟 Large Kafka Messages → Broker Crash

### Anti-pattern

Kafka used as blob transport.

### What happened

* Broker memory pressure
* GC thrashing
* ISR shrinks
* Producer timeouts
* Cluster instability

### Seen at

* Logging platforms
* Media pipelines

### Lesson

> **Kafka scales metadata, not blobs.**

---

## 1️⃣1️⃣ No Backpressure → OOM Crash

### Anti-pattern

Unlimited queues.

### What happened

* Traffic spike
* Queue grows silently
* Memory exhausted
* Process killed
* Restart loop

### Seen at

* Async job processors
* REST APIs under load

### Lesson

> **Unbounded queues hide failure until it’s fatal.**

---

## 1️⃣2️⃣ Cold Failover → “It Didn’t Work”

### Anti-pattern

Failover path never exercised.

### What happened

* Primary failed
* Failover misconfigured
* Data stale
* Manual intervention needed

### Seen at

* Databases
* DNS systems
* Control planes

### Lesson

> **Unused paths rot.**

---

## 1️⃣3️⃣ High Cardinality Metrics → Observability Outage

### Anti-pattern

Unbounded labels (userId, requestId).

### What happened

* Metrics backend overloaded
* Dashboards blank
* Blind during outage

### Seen at

* Prometheus at scale
* SaaS observability platforms

### Lesson

> **You can DoS your own monitoring.**

---

## 1️⃣4️⃣ Auto-Scaling Without Warm-Up → Slow Death

### Anti-pattern

Scale reacts after saturation.

### What happened

* Traffic spike
* Instances scale up too late
* Cold caches
* Users experience minutes of slowness

### Seen at

* E-commerce events
* Ticket sales
* Streaming launches

### Lesson

> **Reactive scaling is already late.**

---

## 🧠 Distinguished Engineer Meta-Lesson

Every outage above shares one trait:

> **The system scaled “correctly” — until reality violated an assumption.**

Assumptions that usually fail:

* Uniform traffic
* Independent failures
* Fast retries
* Even data distribution
* Rare edge cases
