# 🚨 DDIA Anti-Patterns → Outages

---

## 1️⃣ **Assuming Uniform Load**

### Anti-pattern

> “We hash by user_id — load should be even.”

### What Actually Happens

* A few users/tenants dominate traffic
* One shard / partition melts
* Others idle

### Outage Signature

* One node at 100% CPU
* Growing tail latency
* Scaling doesn’t help

### DDIA Lesson

* **Skew beats volume**
* Uniform hashing is an assumption, not a guarantee

---

## 2️⃣ **Global Coordination in the Hot Path**

### Anti-pattern

> “We’ll just make it strongly consistent.”

### What Actually Happens

* Locks, consensus, or 2PC in request path
* Latency spikes
* Throughput collapses under load

### Outage Signature

* P99 explodes
* Throughput flatlines
* Cascading timeouts

### DDIA Lesson

* Coordination kills scalability
* Strong consistency is a latency tax

---

## 3️⃣ **Treating Distributed Systems as Reliable**

### Anti-pattern

> “Nodes rarely fail.”

### What Actually Happens

* Partial failure
* Slow nodes, packet loss, GC pauses

### Outage Signature

* Requests hang
* No clear errors
* Thread pools exhausted

### DDIA Lesson

* Partial failure is normal
* Timeouts are correctness mechanisms

---

## 4️⃣ **Retry Storms**

### Anti-pattern

> “Just retry on failure.”

### What Actually Happens

* Dependency slows
* Clients retry simultaneously
* Load multiplies

### Outage Signature

* Error rate increases after recovery
* Traffic spikes during incident
* Self-inflicted DDoS

### DDIA Lesson

* Retries amplify failure
* Backoff and jitter are mandatory

---

## 5️⃣ **Cache as a Band-Aid**

### Anti-pattern

> “We’ll fix latency with caching.”

### What Actually Happens

* Cache invalidation bugs
* Cache stampede on expiry
* Stale or inconsistent data

### Outage Signature

* DB overwhelmed on cache miss
* Thundering herd
* Sudden latency cliffs

### DDIA Lesson

* Caches trade correctness for performance
* Every cache introduces new failure modes

---

## 6️⃣ **Ignoring Tail Latency**

### Anti-pattern

> “Average latency is fine.”

### What Actually Happens

* Long tail grows unnoticed
* Users experience timeouts

### Outage Signature

* Complaints despite “green dashboards”
* Slow degradation before incident

### DDIA Lesson

* Averages lie
* Tail latency defines user experience

---

## 7️⃣ **Unbounded Queues**

### Anti-pattern

> “Queues will absorb spikes.”

### What Actually Happens

* Queue grows indefinitely
* Memory or disk exhaustion
* Delayed failures

### Outage Signature

* Lag keeps growing
* System looks healthy until it crashes

### DDIA Lesson

* Backpressure must be explicit
* Buffers delay failure, not prevent it

---

## 8️⃣ **Synchronous Cross-Service Calls**

### Anti-pattern

> “Service A just calls Service B.”

### What Actually Happens

* Latency stacks
* Failure propagates

### Outage Signature

* Cascading timeouts
* Large blast radius

### DDIA Lesson

* Synchronous calls propagate failure
* Asynchrony contains blast radius

---

## 9️⃣ **Overusing Exactly-Once Semantics**

### Anti-pattern

> “Exactly-once guarantees correctness.”

### What Actually Happens

* Throughput drops
* Control plane overloaded
* Failures harder to recover

### Outage Signature

* Consumers stall
* Rebalances frequent
* Latency spikes on recovery

### DDIA Lesson

* Exactly-once is expensive
* Idempotency scales better

---

## 🔟 **Manual Failover Assumptions**

### Anti-pattern

> “We’ll just fail over.”

### What Actually Happens

* Stale reads
* Lost writes
* Split-brain behavior

### Outage Signature

* Data inconsistency reports
* Conflicting states
* Long recovery

### DDIA Lesson

* Failover trades availability for correctness
* Replication lag matters

---

## 1️⃣1️⃣ **No Migration Strategy**

### Anti-pattern

> “We’ll re-shard later.”

### What Actually Happens

* Data stuck on hot shard
* Downtime during migration

### Outage Signature

* Emergency resharding
* Risky manual ops
* Prolonged incident

### DDIA Lesson

* Migration cost dominates design
* Shard keys are irreversible

---

## 1️⃣2️⃣ **Schema Changes Without Compatibility**

### Anti-pattern

> “Deploy producer first.”

### What Actually Happens

* Old consumers break
* Data unreadable

### Outage Signature

* Serialization errors
* Consumer crashes
* Data loss risk

### DDIA Lesson

* Independent deploys require compatibility
* Schema evolution is coordination

---

## 1️⃣3️⃣ **Time-Based Assumptions**

### Anti-pattern

> “Timestamps define order.”

### What Actually Happens

* Clock skew
* Reordered events

### Outage Signature

* Negative latencies
* Incorrect ordering

### DDIA Lesson

* Time is unreliable
* Causality beats clocks

---

## 1️⃣4️⃣ **Consensus Everywhere**

### Anti-pattern

> “Let’s use consensus for safety.”

### What Actually Happens

* Latency spikes
* Availability drops during leader changes

### Outage Signature

* System stalls during elections
* Reduced throughput

### DDIA Lesson

* Consensus is for metadata
* Data paths should avoid it

---

## 1️⃣5️⃣ **Optimizing for the Happy Path**

### Anti-pattern

> “This works in staging.”

### What Actually Happens

* Rare failures become catastrophic

### Outage Signature

* Unhandled edge cases
* Undefined behavior

### DDIA Lesson

* Failure handling defines correctness
* Happy paths are easy

---

> “Most outages aren’t caused by bugs — they’re caused by assumptions that stop being true at scale.”
