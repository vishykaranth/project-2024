# 🧠 TOP 100 SCALABILITY QUESTIONS & ANSWERS

---

## I. First Principles (1–10)

**1. What is scalability?**
Ability to handle increased load by adding resources *without redesign*.

**2. Scale up vs scale out?**
Up: bigger machines (limited). Out: more machines (preferred).

**3. What usually breaks first when scaling?**
Coordination points, not CPU.

**4. Why does adding nodes sometimes reduce throughput?**
Increased coordination and contention.

**5. Vertical scaling limit?**
Hardware ceiling and single failure domain.

**6. Horizontal scaling limit?**
State, coordination, and data locality.

**7. What is a bottleneck?**
The slowest shared dependency in the critical path.

**8. Can you scale reads infinitely?**
Only if data consistency requirements allow it.

**9. Why is latency harder than throughput?**
Tail latencies compound across services.

**10. What is Amdahl’s Law?**
Serial work limits parallel speedup.

---

## II. Bottlenecks & Coordination (11–25)

**11. What creates bottlenecks?**
Shared mutable state.

**12. Why do locks kill scalability?**
They serialize progress.

**13. Centralized ID generators — problem?**
Global serialization on every write.

**14. Why is leader-based architecture risky?**
Leader caps throughput and availability.

**15. How do you remove coordination?**
Partition ownership or embed uniqueness in data.

**16. When is coordination unavoidable?**
Money, correctness, safety.

**17. What’s worse: slow dependency or flaky one?**
Flaky — causes retries and storms.

**18. Why retries amplify failures?**
They increase load during degradation.

**19. What is backpressure?**
A signal to slow producers before collapse.

**20. What happens without backpressure?**
Queues grow → memory exhaustion → crash.

**21. Why is global ordering expensive?**
Requires synchronization across nodes.

**22. When is eventual consistency acceptable?**
When correctness can lag but converge.

**23. How do you spot hidden bottlenecks?**
Per-resource saturation metrics.

**24. What is convoy effect?**
Slow operation blocks many fast ones.

**25. Why do “small” services cause big outages?**
They sit on the critical path.

---

## III. Data & Storage (26–40)

**26. Why does sharding scale writes?**
Removes write contention.

**27. Hardest part of sharding?**
Re-sharding later.

**28. What causes hot shards?**
Skewed access patterns.

**29. Hash vs range sharding?**
Hash: balance. Range: locality.

**30. Why do auto-increment keys hurt?**
Create write hotspots.

**31. What breaks DB replicas?**
Replication lag.

**32. Why is metadata often the bottleneck?**
High fan-out, low cacheability.

**33. Indexes vs scalability?**
Indexes speed reads but slow writes.

**34. When does NoSQL outperform RDBMS?**
High write scale with simple queries.

**35. When does RDBMS win?**
Strong consistency and complex joins.

**36. Why is write amplification bad?**
Consumes I/O disproportionately.

**37. What is compaction cost?**
Hidden background write load.

**38. Why do large tables degrade faster?**
Cache miss rate increases.

**39. Why is schema evolution hard at scale?**
Coordinated deploys are risky.

**40. Best way to delete at scale?**
Tombstones + async cleanup.

---

## IV. Caching (41–55)

**41. What problem does caching solve?**
Reduces load on slower dependencies.

**42. Cache-aside vs write-through?**
Cache-aside gives flexibility; write-through gives consistency.

**43. Cache stampede?**
Many clients miss and overload backend.

**44. How to prevent stampede?**
TTL jitter, request coalescing.

**45. What is stale cache risk?**
Serving outdated data.

**46. Why is cache invalidation hard?**
Distributed timing uncertainty.

**47. What should never be cached?**
Strongly consistent writes.

**48. Why do caches increase blast radius?**
Shared dependency failure.

**49. Local cache vs distributed?**
Local is faster, distributed is consistent.

**50. Why does cache hit ratio lie?**
Misses may be more expensive.

**51. What’s negative caching?**
Caching “not found” responses.

**52. Why TTL alone is insufficient?**
Doesn’t handle write bursts.

**53. Cache warm-up problem?**
Cold start overloads backend.

**54. What is cache poisoning?**
Bad data spreading system-wide.

**55. Why cache size isn’t the solution?**
Skew dominates.

---

## V. Messaging & Streaming (56–70)

**56. Why use queues?**
Decouple producers and consumers.

**57. Kafka’s scalability unit?**
Partition.

**58. Why can’t you parallelize one partition?**
Ordering guarantees.

**59. What causes consumer lag?**
Slow processing or hot partitions.

**60. Why adding consumers doesn’t help?**
Partition limit.

**61. Exactly-once — myth?**
Expensive and fragile.

**62. At-least-once — risk?**
Duplicates.

**63. How to handle duplicates?**
Idempotent consumers.

**64. Why do retries break Kafka?**
Reprocessing storms.

**65. What’s backpressure in Kafka?**
Lag growth signals overload.

**66. Why is rebalancing painful?**
State movement + pause.

**67. What causes hot keys?**
Skewed producer keys.

**68. How to fix hot partitions?**
Key redesign or migration.

**69. Why is offset management critical?**
Data loss or duplication risk.

**70. Why logs scale better than queues?**
Immutable append-only.

---

## VI. Reliability & Failure (71–85)

**71. What is a cascading failure?**
Failure spreads across services.

**72. Why timeouts matter?**
Prevent thread exhaustion.

**73. Why default timeouts are dangerous?**
Too long, hide failures.

**74. Circuit breaker purpose?**
Fail fast under load.

**75. Why partial failures are worse?**
Harder to detect.

**76. What is bulkheading?**
Isolating failure domains.

**77. Why health checks lie?**
They don’t test real load.

**78. Why deploys cause outages?**
Cache invalidation + warm-up.

**79. Why retries need limits?**
Prevent retry storms.

**80. What is load shedding?**
Drop work to save system.

**81. Why graceful degradation matters?**
Preserves core functionality.

**82. Why chaos testing helps?**
Finds unknown failure modes.

**83. Why redundancy isn’t enough?**
Shared dependencies fail together.

**84. Why failovers fail?**
Cold paths rot.

**85. What causes brownouts?**
Resource saturation.

---

## VII. Observability & Ops (86–100)

**86. What do you measure first?**
Saturation.

**87. Why averages lie?**
Tail latency matters.

**88. What is SLO?**
User-centric reliability target.

**89. Error budget purpose?**
Balance speed vs stability.

**90. Why dashboards fail?**
Too many metrics.

**91. Golden signals?**
Latency, traffic, errors, saturation.

**92. Why alerts fatigue teams?**
Too many non-actionable alerts.

**93. Why logs don’t scale?**
Volume explosion.

**94. Metrics vs logs?**
Metrics scale; logs explain.

**95. Why tracing is expensive?**
High cardinality.

**96. What is cardinality explosion?**
Unbounded label values.

**97. Why capacity planning fails?**
Traffic isn’t linear.

**98. Why autoscaling lags?**
Reactive, not predictive.

**99. Why oncall maturity matters?**
Design improves reliability.

**100. Ultimate scalability rule?**
Avoid global coordination.
