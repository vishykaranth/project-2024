This is **not** a template.

Use it in:

* system design interviews

* pre-launch readiness

# System Design Decision Checklist (Principal+)

> **Goal:** Surface hidden assumptions, bottlenecks, and irreversible decisions *before production does it for you.*

---

## 1️⃣ Problem Framing (Start Here or Don’t Start)

* What problem are we *actually* solving?
* What happens if we do nothing?
* Is this latency-sensitive, throughput-sensitive, or correctness-sensitive?
* What is the success metric *users* care about?
* What can be wrong and still acceptable?
* What must never be wrong?
* What’s the expected growth curve (users, QPS, data)?
* Which assumptions will break first?

**Principal smell**

> “We’re designing a system without agreeing on what failure looks like.”

---

## 2️⃣ Workload & Access Patterns

* Read-heavy, write-heavy, or bursty?
* What’s the read/write ratio at P50 vs P99?
* Are there natural hotspots?
* Is access evenly distributed?
* What’s the worst-case request?
* Are traffic patterns time-based (cron, batch, peaks)?
* What’s the fan-out per request?
* Which paths are latency-critical?

**Smell**

> “Average load hides tail pain.”

---

## 3️⃣ IDs & Keys (Silent System Shapers)

* How are IDs generated?
* What failure mode does this ID strategy choose?
* Is ordering required?
* Is global uniqueness required?
* Does ID structure affect partitioning?
* Can we migrate this ID scheme later?
* What happens under clock skew?
* Do IDs create hot partitions?

**Smell**

> “This ID choice locks us into future pain.”

---

## 4️⃣ Data Model & Storage

* What is the primary data model?
* What are the dominant query patterns?
* What’s the primary key and why?
* Where will indexes hurt writes?
* What’s the largest record?
* How does data grow over time?
* What’s the compaction / cleanup story?
* Can we change the schema online?

**Smell**

> “The data model optimizes the wrong thing.”

---

## 5️⃣ Partitioning & Sharding

* What is the partition key?
* How evenly does it distribute load?
* What happens when one key becomes hot?
* Can we reshard online?
* How many partitions today vs in 2 years?
* What’s the rebalancing cost?
* Do cross-partition queries exist?
* What is the blast radius of a bad partition?

**Smell**

> “This design assumes uniform traffic.”

---

## 6️⃣ Consistency & Correctness

* What consistency guarantees are required?
* Where is eventual consistency acceptable?
* Can users see stale data?
* Is read-your-writes required?
* How do we handle conflicts?
* What happens during partial failure?
* What correctness bugs are possible?
* How do we detect them?

**Smell**

> “We’re hand-waving correctness.”

---

## 7️⃣ Caching Strategy

* Why are we caching?
* What happens on cache miss?
* What’s the TTL and why?
* How do we invalidate?
* What happens during cache warmup?
* Can cache failure take down the system?
* Do hot keys exist?
* How does cache affect tail latency?

**Smell**

> “The cache hides a design flaw.”

---

## 8️⃣ Messaging / Async (Kafka, Queues)

* Why async instead of sync?
* What ordering guarantees exist?
* What happens if consumers lag?
* What happens if one partition is hot?
* Is reprocessing required?
* How are offsets managed?
* What’s the DLQ strategy?
* How do we replay safely?

**Smell**

> “Async is masking backpressure.”

---

## 9️⃣ APIs & Dependencies

* What are our dependencies?
* What happens when they slow down?
* What’s the timeout and retry strategy?
* Do retries amplify load?
* Is the API idempotent?
* What versioning strategy exists?
* How many downstream calls per request?
* What’s the worst-case fan-out?

**Smell**

> “We’re trusting downstream systems too much.”

---

## 🔟 Scaling Strategy

* What’s the first bottleneck?
* What’s the second?
* Can we scale reads independently of writes?
* Can we scale partitions independently?
* What breaks at 10× traffic?
* What breaks at 100×?
* What’s the operational cost of scaling?
* What can’t be scaled horizontally?

**Smell**

> “We don’t know our ceiling.”

---

## 1️⃣1️⃣ Failure Modes & Resilience

* What fails when a node dies?
* What fails when a zone dies?
* What fails when a region dies?
* How does the system degrade?
* Is there graceful degradation?
* What’s the recovery time?
* Is failover tested?
* Can humans recover it at 3am?

**Smell**

> “We only designed for the happy path.”

---

## 1️⃣2️⃣ Observability & Operability

* What metrics define health?
* What does a bad day look like in dashboards?
* Can we detect hot partitions?
* Can we detect lag early?
* What logs matter?
* Are alerts actionable?
* Can oncall diagnose quickly?
* What’s the rollback plan?

**Smell**

> “We’ll figure it out in production.”

---

## 1️⃣3️⃣ Migration & Evolution

* How do we change this later?
* Can we run old + new in parallel?
* How do we migrate data safely?
* What happens mid-migration failure?
* Can we roll back?
* How long will migration take?
* Who owns migration success?
* What’s the kill switch?

**Smell**

> “There’s no migration story.”

---

## 1️⃣4️⃣ Security & Multi-Tenancy

* How is data isolated?
* What’s the tenant boundary?
* Are noisy neighbors possible?
* How is access controlled?
* What happens on auth failure?
* Is encryption required?
* What’s the key rotation story?
* Can security failures cascade?

**Smell**

> “Security is bolted on.”

---

## 1️⃣5️⃣ Cost & Capacity

* What’s the biggest cost driver?
* How does cost scale with traffic?
* What happens under abuse?
* Are we paying for idle capacity?
* What’s the cost of redundancy?
* Can we predict spend growth?
* What’s the cost of failure?
* Is cost visibility built in?

**Smell**

> “We don’t know what this costs at scale.”

---

## 1️⃣6️⃣ Human Factors (The Part Everyone Skips)

* Who will operate this?
* Who will debug it?
* Who will get paged?
* What’s the oncall load?
* Is tribal knowledge required?
* Can new engineers reason about it?
* What happens during handoff?
* Is simplicity valued?

**Smell**

> “This only works if Alice is around.”

---

## Final Principal Rule

> **If you can’t clearly state the failure modes you’re choosing, you’re not done designing.**

---

## How Distinguished Engineers Use This

They don’t answer every question out loud.

They **listen** — and jump in when:

* an irreversible choice is being made
* a failure mode is unowned
* a migration path is missing
* a hotspot is ignored
