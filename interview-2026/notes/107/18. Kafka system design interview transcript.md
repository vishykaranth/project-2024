# 🎤 Kafka System Design Interview — Transcript

### **Interviewer**

Design a Kafka-based system to process real-time payments events at scale.

---

## 1️⃣ Clarifying & Framing (Principal move)

**Candidate**

> “Before jumping into Kafka, I want to clarify the invariants.”

> “What’s the correctness requirement — ordering, duplication tolerance, latency SLO?”

> “Do we care about per-user ordering or global ordering?”

> “What’s the expected skew? Payments systems usually have hot merchants.”

> “What’s the replay and migration expectation?”

**Interviewer**
We need per-user ordering, at-least-once delivery, sub-second latency, and replays for audits.

---

## 2️⃣ High-Level Design (No diagrams yet)

**Candidate**

> “Kafka fits well because this is an append-only event stream with replay requirements.”

> “Partitions will define our parallelism ceiling.”

> “The partition key choice will dominate scalability.”

> “At-least-once with idempotent consumers is the right trade-off here.”

---

## 3️⃣ Topic & Partition Strategy

**Candidate**

> “I’d start with a `payments-events` topic.”

> “Partitioning by `user_id` gives us per-user ordering.”

> “But this assumes user traffic is uniformly distributed — which is usually false.”

> “We should expect hot users and hot merchants.”

> “So we need a mitigation strategy before this becomes a production incident.”

**Interviewer**
What mitigation strategy?

---

## 4️⃣ Hot Partition Strategy (Where Seniority Shows)

**Candidate**

> “We can’t out-scale a hot partition.”

> “Adding consumers won’t help because partitions cap concurrency.”

> “I’d introduce **key indirection** early.”

> “Instead of partitioning directly on `user_id`, we partition on `hash(user_id + bucket)`.”

> “Bucket count is configurable and allows us to fan out hot users.”

> “This trades strict per-user ordering for bounded ordering within buckets.”

> “We need to confirm if that’s acceptable.”

**Interviewer**
What if strict ordering is mandatory?

**Candidate**

> “Then the system fundamentally doesn’t scale linearly.”

> “We should be explicit about that constraint.”

> “We can isolate hot users into dedicated topics.”

> “Operationally expensive, but it preserves correctness.”

---

## 5️⃣ Consumers & Processing

**Candidate**

> “Consumers will be stateless and horizontally scalable.”

> “Processing must be idempotent because at-least-once guarantees duplicates.”

> “We should avoid synchronous downstream calls.”

> “Any external IO introduces backpressure and lag amplification.”

> “Retries must have exponential backoff — otherwise we’ll DDoS ourselves.”

---

## 6️⃣ Lag & Backpressure Discussion

**Interviewer**
How do you handle consumer lag?

**Candidate**

> “Lag is a signal, not the root problem.”

> “I’d alert on **lag growth rate per partition**, not total lag.”

> “One partition lagging is more dangerous than total lag increasing.”

> “We need per-partition visibility to detect hot keys early.”

> “Kafka will absorb spikes, but disk is the real backpressure.”

---

## 7️⃣ Failure Modes (Explicit Callouts)

**Candidate**

> “Rebalances are coordinated pauses.”

> “Deploys will cause temporary lag.”

> “Exactly-once would increase blast radius under failure.”

> “Large messages could destabilize brokers.”

> “Retry storms can turn a partial outage into a full outage.”

> “Disk saturation is a slow-burning failure.”

---

## 8️⃣ Replay & Auditing

**Interviewer**
How would you handle replays?

**Candidate**

> “Consumers must be replay-safe.”

> “Replays should be isolated — ideally using shadow consumer groups.”

> “Offsets should never be manually reset in production pipelines.”

> “Replays can overload downstream systems if not rate-limited.”

> “We need a replay kill switch.”

---

## 9️⃣ Metrics & Observability

**Candidate**

> “Kafka broker metrics alone are insufficient.”

> “We need:
>
> * Lag per partition
> * Processing time per message
> * Key distribution histograms
> * Rebalance frequency
> * ISR shrink events”

> “Aggregate metrics hide skew.”

---

## 🔟 Migration & Evolution

**Interviewer**
What if traffic grows 10x?

**Candidate**

> “Partition count sets our scaling ceiling.”

> “Increasing partitions is possible but operationally expensive.”

> “Key evolution requires dual-writing and replay.”

> “We need a migration story before scale forces it.”

> “Most Kafka failures happen during migrations, not steady state.”

---

## 1️⃣1️⃣ Closing Summary (Principal Finish)

**Candidate**

> “Kafka gives us durability and replay.”

> “Partitioning determines scalability.”

> “Hot keys are the dominant risk.”

> “Lag is an early warning, not a metric to suppress.”

> “Operational simplicity beats theoretical throughput.”
