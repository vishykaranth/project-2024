# 🧠 DDIA Cheat-Sheet for Interviews (Principal / Distinguished)

Use these as **mental anchors + verbal one-liners**.

---

## 1️⃣ Core DDIA Worldview (Say this early)

* “State is the hardest part of distributed systems.”
* “Coordination is the real scalability limit.”
* “Skew breaks systems faster than volume.”
* “Most failures are partial, not total.”
* “Operational reality matters more than theoretical guarantees.”

---

## 2️⃣ Reliability

**Think**

* Fault ≠ failure
* Partial failure is normal

**Say**

* “I assume components fail independently and unpredictably.”
* “Timeouts turn slow failures into fast ones.”
* “Fail fast beats fail silent.”
* “Retries without backoff amplify outages.”

---

## 3️⃣ Scalability

**Think**

* Growth, not size
* Tail latency > average

**Say**

* “Averages lie — tail latency defines user experience.”
* “Throughput scales until coordination appears.”
* “One hot key dominates capacity.”
* “Scaling is about removing bottlenecks, not adding machines.”

---

## 4️⃣ Maintainability

**Think**

* Systems outlive teams
* Humans are part of the system

**Say**

* “Simple systems fail predictably.”
* “Operational complexity compounds.”
* “Explicit trade-offs beat implicit assumptions.”
* “The best design is the one that’s easy to operate.”

---

## 5️⃣ Data Models

**Think**

* Access patterns drive schema

**Say**

* “The data model encodes system assumptions.”
* “You can’t add joins cheaply later.”
* “Schema design is performance design.”
* “Flexibility today becomes ambiguity tomorrow.”

---

## 6️⃣ Storage Engines

**Think**

* Append > update
* Background work matters

**Say**

* “Write amplification always shows up later.”
* “Compaction is deferred cost.”
* “Sequential IO wins until it doesn’t.”
* “Background maintenance becomes foreground pain under load.”

---

## 7️⃣ Encoding & Schema Evolution

**Think**

* Independent deploys

**Say**

* “Backward compatibility is non-negotiable.”
* “Producers and consumers evolve independently.”
* “Schema mistakes are forever.”
* “Never assume synchronized deploys.”

---

## 8️⃣ Replication

**Think**

* Availability vs consistency

**Say**

* “Replication lag is user-visible inconsistency.”
* “Failover always risks data loss or staleness.”
* “Multi-leader trades availability for conflict resolution.”
* “Consistency is a latency choice.”

---

## 9️⃣ Partitioning / Sharding

**Think**

* Parallelism + failure domains

**Say**

* “Partitioning defines scalability limits.”
* “Hashing assumes uniform traffic.”
* “Hot partitions negate horizontal scale.”
* “Shard keys are long-term contracts.”

---

## 🔟 Transactions

**Think**

* Coordination cost

**Say**

* “Distributed transactions don’t fail cleanly.”
* “Two-phase commit trades simplicity for illusion.”
* “Idempotency scales better than atomicity.”
* “Compensation beats coordination.”

---

## 1️⃣1️⃣ Consistency Models

**Think**

* User expectations

**Say**

* “Linearizability doesn’t scale globally.”
* “Causal consistency is often enough.”
* “Eventual consistency shifts complexity to applications.”
* “Strong consistency increases tail latency.”

---

## 1️⃣2️⃣ Consensus

**Think**

* Control plane only

**Say**

* “Consensus is expensive — use sparingly.”
* “It belongs in metadata, not data paths.”
* “Leader election is a latency spike.”
* “Availability drops during reconfiguration.”

---

## 1️⃣3️⃣ Batch vs Stream

**Think**

* Accuracy vs latency

**Say**

* “Batch is simpler and reproducible.”
* “Streams are databases in motion.”
* “Stateful streams inherit database problems.”
* “Exactly-once is a coordination tax.”

---

## 1️⃣4️⃣ Time & Ordering

**Think**

* Clocks lie

**Say**

* “Time is unreliable in distributed systems.”
* “Ordering is expensive.”
* “Causality beats timestamps.”
* “Clock drift creates anomalies.”

---

## 1️⃣5️⃣ Failure Patterns (Red Flags)

**Call these out**

* “This introduces global coordination.”
* “This assumes uniform load.”
* “We don’t have a migration story.”
* “Retries could amplify failure.”
* “This hides backpressure.”
* “What happens when one partition is hot?”

---

## 1️⃣6️⃣ Metrics That Matter

**Say**

* “Tail latency over averages.”
* “Lag growth rate, not absolute lag.”
* “Skew indicators.”
* “Error rate by dependency.”
* “Rebalance frequency.”

---

## 1️⃣7️⃣ Migration & Evolution

**Think**

* Change is constant

**Say**

* “Migration cost dominates design choice.”
* “Dual-write before cutover.”
* “Replays must be idempotent.”
* “Every migration needs rollback.”

---

## 1️⃣8️⃣ How to Close Any Answer (Magic Line)

Pick one:

* “This is a trade-off between consistency and availability.”
* “The real risk here is skew.”
* “This design fails under partial failure.”
* “Operational complexity is the limiting factor.”
* “We should be explicit about what breaks first.”

---

## 🧠 30-Second DDIA Summary (Memorize This)

> “DDIA teaches that distributed systems fail because of hidden assumptions about data, time, and coordination. Scalability is limited by skew and coordination, not hardware. Strong guarantees increase latency and reduce availability. Systems that survive make trade-offs explicit, minimize coordination, design for partial failure, and optimize for operability over theoretical correctness.”
