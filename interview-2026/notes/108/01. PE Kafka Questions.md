# 🔥 Kafka Tough Interview Questions (Principal Engineer)

---

## 1. Why does adding consumers not reduce lag?

**What they want**

> Understanding of partition parallelism.

**Principal answer**

> Because Kafka parallelism is bounded by partitions. If lag is isolated to one partition, adding consumers only increases idle capacity. This smells like a hot key or skewed partitioning problem, not consumer throughput.

---

## 2. How do you detect a hot partition *before* lag explodes?

**Signal**

> Proactive observability.

**Principal answer**

> I monitor per-partition produce rate, fetch time, and consumer lag variance—not averages. A rising max-to-median ratio across partitions is an early warning.

---

## 3. What happens if a consumer crashes after processing but before committing?

**Signal**

> Delivery semantics.

**Principal answer**

> You get reprocessing. Kafka guarantees at-least-once by default; correctness comes from idempotent consumers, not commit timing.

---

## 4. Why can Kafka appear “slow” even when brokers are healthy?

**Signal**

> End-to-end thinking.

**Principal answer**

> GC pauses, ISR shrink, disk IO saturation, controller churn, or network throttling. Kafka’s bottleneck is often outside the broker metrics dashboard.

---

## 5. Explain exactly how ISR shrink causes tail latency

**Signal**

> Replication internals.

**Principal answer**

> When followers fall out of ISR, leaders block acks waiting for in-sync replicas. That increases produce latency and amplifies retries, pushing tail latency up system-wide.

---

## 6. Why is `acks=all` sometimes faster than `acks=1`?

**Signal**

> Counterintuitive systems thinking.

**Principal answer**

> `acks=all` reduces retries under failure by waiting for quorum. Fewer retries often beats faster individual sends.

---

## 7. What failure does Kafka retries amplify?

**Signal**

> Load amplification awareness.

**Principal answer**

> Transient broker slowdowns. Retries turn latency into throughput collapse and create feedback loops.

---

## 8. How can consumer lag be zero while messages are delayed?

**Signal**

> Misleading metrics.

**Principal answer**

> Consumers may commit offsets early or buffer messages internally. Lag measures position, not processing completion.

---

## 9. Why does rebalancing hurt large consumer groups?

**Signal**

> Operational pain.

**Principal answer**

> Cooperative rebalancing still pauses partitions. With large groups, even seconds of pause multiply into visible lag spikes.

---

## 10. When would you *not* use Kafka transactions?

**Signal**

> Cost awareness.

**Principal answer**

> High-throughput pipelines where idempotency is enough. Transactions add coordinator load and increase tail latency.

---

## 11. Explain a Kafka outage caused by ZooKeeper / KRaft

**Signal**

> Control plane awareness.

**Principal answer**

> Controller elections stall metadata updates. Leaders stop serving writes, even though brokers are alive. Data plane waits on control plane.

---

## 12. Why is increasing partitions risky in production?

**Signal**

> Irreversibility.

**Principal answer**

> Partition count is mostly irreversible. It increases open files, memory, rebalance cost, and operational complexity permanently.

---

## 13. How do you migrate a hot key without downtime?

**Signal**

> Real migration strategy.

**Principal answer**

> Dual-write with new partitioning, shadow consume, validate parity, then cut over. Never move a hot key live.

---

## 14. Why does Kafka prefer sequential IO but still suffer on SSDs?

**Signal**

> Storage internals.

**Principal answer**

> Log compaction, index scans, and page cache misses introduce random IO patterns even on SSD.

---

## 15. What causes consumer fetch latency spikes?

**Signal**

> Broker internals.

**Principal answer**

> GC pauses, leader disk stalls, ISR sync delays, or fetch wait time misconfiguration.

---

## 16. Explain “exactly-once” in Kafka without marketing language

**Signal**

> Clarity.

**Principal answer**

> Kafka provides atomic write + offset commit per producer. It prevents duplicates inside Kafka, not in downstream systems.

---

## 17. Why do compacted topics still grow unbounded?

**Signal**

> Reality check.

**Principal answer**

> Tombstones, retention windows, key churn, and delayed compaction mean disk usage lags logical size.

---

## 18. What metrics tell you brokers are about to fall over?

**Signal**

> Early warning instincts.

**Principal answer**

> GC pause frequency, request queue depth, ISR shrink rate, disk utilization slope—not CPU.

---

## 19. How do you protect Kafka from downstream slowness?

**Signal**

> Backpressure thinking.

**Principal answer**

> Rate limit producers, cap retention, enforce quotas, and design consumers to shed load rather than retry.

---

## 20. What’s the most common Kafka anti-pattern?

**Signal**

> Experience.

**Principal answer**

> Treating Kafka as a queue instead of a log—especially relying on lag as a correctness metric.

---

## 21. Explain how Kafka causes cascading failures

**Signal**

> System-wide thinking.

**Principal answer**

> Slow consumers → lag → retries → broker pressure → ISR shrink → producer retries → cluster saturation.

---

## 22. Why are small messages sometimes worse than large ones?

**Signal**

> Throughput math.

**Principal answer**

> Per-message overhead dominates. Batching efficiency collapses.

---

## 23. When do you scale brokers vs partitions?

**Signal**

> Capacity planning.

**Principal answer**

> Scale brokers for IO and memory; scale partitions for parallelism. They solve different bottlenecks.

---

## 24. Why does consumer lag explode after deployments?

**Signal**

> Real-world scars.

**Principal answer**

> Rebalancing pauses + cold caches + JVM warm-up. Lag spike is expected unless mitigated.

---

## 25. What does “Kafka is backpressured” actually mean?

**Signal**

> Precision.

**Principal answer**

> Brokers are accepting writes but increasing latency, which shifts pressure upstream via retries.

---

## 26. How do you design partition keys safely?

**Signal**

> Preventive thinking.

**Principal answer**

> Keys must be bounded, evenly distributed, and evolvable. If you can’t migrate them, they’re wrong.

---

## 27. Why is max.poll.interval.ms dangerous?

**Signal**

> Consumer internals.

**Principal answer**

> Long processing pauses trigger rebalances, duplicating work and increasing lag.

---

## 28. Explain Kafka vs Pulsar tradeoff at PE level

**Signal**

> Neutral judgment.

**Principal answer**

> Kafka optimizes for throughput and simplicity; Pulsar for multi-tenancy and isolation. Operational model matters more than features.

---

## 29. What breaks first in Kafka under load?

**Signal**

> Hard-earned truth.

**Principal answer**

> Disk, not CPU. Then GC. Then humans.

---

## 30. One Kafka belief you strongly disagree with

**Signal**

> Maturity.

**Principal answer**

> “Just add partitions.” That’s deferring design debt into operations.
