# 🔥 Kafka Outage Postmortems (Principal-Level)

---

## 1. Hot Partition → Full Pipeline Stall

### What happened

* One partition received ~60% of traffic due to skewed key (`userId`)
* Consumer lag grew only on that partition
* Upstream services timed out waiting for downstream processing

### Why it cascaded

* Adding consumers didn’t help (partition bottleneck)
* Retries amplified load on the same hot partition
* Other partitions were idle → false sense of capacity

### Missed signals

* No per-partition throughput dashboards
* Lag alerts were averaged across partitions

### Corrective actions

* Introduced **composite partition key**
* Added **key-level rate limiting**
* Built **hot-key detection using p99 partition skew**

> **Principal takeaway:**
> *Parallelism is bounded by partitions. Load skew is a correctness bug.*

---

## 2. Retry Storm Took Down the Cluster

### What happened

* One broker slowed due to disk IO saturation
* Producers saw latency spikes and retried aggressively
* Broker queues filled, ISR shrank, more retries followed

### Why it cascaded

* Retry policy had no jitter
* All producers retried in sync
* `acks=1` caused silent message loss fears → more retries

### Missed signals

* Retry rate not monitored
* No backpressure on producers

### Corrective actions

* Introduced **exponential backoff with jitter**
* Enforced **producer quotas**
* Switched critical topics to `acks=all`

> **Principal takeaway:**
> *Retries turn latency problems into availability outages.*

---

## 3. Consumer Rebalance Storm After Deploy

### What happened

* Rolling deploy restarted many consumers
* Group rebalanced repeatedly
* Processing paused for minutes

### Why it cascaded

* Large consumer group (200+ instances)
* `max.poll.interval.ms` too low
* Cooperative rebalancing not enabled

### Missed signals

* No alert on rebalance frequency
* Lag spike attributed to “deploy noise”

### Corrective actions

* Enabled **cooperative sticky assignor**
* Reduced group size
* Staggered deployments

> **Principal takeaway:**
> *Rebalancing is a stop-the-world event disguised as housekeeping.*

---

## 4. “Kafka Is Down” — Actually GC Pauses

### What happened

* Producers timed out
* Consumers disconnected
* Brokers appeared healthy

### Root cause

* One broker experienced long Full GCs
* Heartbeats missed → partitions re-elected
* Leadership churn spiked latency cluster-wide

### Missed signals

* GC logs not centrally monitored
* JVM pauses mistaken for network issues

### Corrective actions

* Switched to **G1 with pause targets**
* Reduced heap fragmentation
* Alerted on **GC pause duration**, not heap usage

> **Principal takeaway:**
> *GC pauses look exactly like network partitions.*

---

## 5. Log Compaction Melted Disk IO

### What happened

* Compacted topic disk usage exploded
* Brokers became IO-bound
* Produce latency spiked globally

### Root cause

* High churn of keys
* Tombstones retained too long
* Compaction lagged behind write rate

### Missed signals

* No compaction lag monitoring
* Assumed compaction = bounded disk

### Corrective actions

* Reduced tombstone retention
* Split topic by key domain
* Reserved IO budget for compaction

> **Principal takeaway:**
> *Compaction is eventual — disk pressure is immediate.*

---

## 6. Controller Election Caused Write Freeze

### What happened

* All producers blocked briefly
* No broker crash observed

### Root cause

* ZooKeeper session expiration
* Controller election stalled metadata updates
* Leaders paused accepting writes

### Missed signals

* ZK latency not monitored
* Control plane treated as “out of band”

### Corrective actions

* Dedicated ZK ensemble
* Lowered metadata churn
* Alerted on controller change rate

> **Principal takeaway:**
> *Kafka’s data plane waits on its control plane.*

---

## 7. “Zero Lag” but Data Was Hours Late

### What happened

* Dashboards showed zero consumer lag
* Business data delayed by hours

### Root cause

* Consumer committed offsets before processing
* Downstream DB was throttling writes
* Messages buffered in-memory

### Missed signals

* Lag confused with processing completion
* No end-to-end latency metric

### Corrective actions

* Commit offsets **after processing**
* Added processing-time SLO
* Backpressure on downstream writes

> **Principal takeaway:**
> *Lag measures position, not correctness.*

---

## 8. Partition Explosion Broke the Cluster

### What happened

* Partitions increased from 1k → 10k
* Broker memory and file descriptors exhausted
* Rebalances took hours

### Root cause

* “Add partitions to scale” advice followed blindly
* No broker-level capacity planning

### Missed signals

* No alert on open file count
* Metadata size growth ignored

### Corrective actions

* Reduced partition count
* Added brokers before partitions
* Partition count treated as irreversible

> **Principal takeaway:**
> *Partitions are not free. They are permanent operational debt.*

---

## 9. ISR Shrink Caused Latency Cliff

### What happened

* Produce latency jumped from 20ms → 3s
* No obvious broker failures

### Root cause

* One slow follower fell out of ISR
* Leaders blocked waiting for replicas
* Tail latency exploded

### Missed signals

* ISR size not alerting
* Focused only on broker health

### Corrective actions

* Alert on ISR shrink rate
* Faster disks for followers
* Reduced replica count for hot topics

> **Principal takeaway:**
> *Replication failures surface as latency, not errors.*

---

## 10. Kafka Became the Blast Radius

### What happened

* Downstream service slowed
* Kafka backlog grew
* Upstream APIs started failing

### Root cause

* Kafka used as a buffer without limits
* Retention too large
* No producer throttling

### Missed signals

* No backlog growth alarms
* Kafka assumed “infinite buffer”

### Corrective actions

* Enforced retention caps
* Rate-limited producers
* Load-shed at ingress

> **Principal takeaway:**
> *Kafka absorbs pressure until it becomes the failure.*
