# My BIGGEST REGRETS From A 40 Year Software Engineering Career

## Overview

Reflecting on a 40-year software engineering career reveals valuable lessons learned the hard way. This summary explores common regrets and mistakes that experienced developers wish they could change, providing wisdom for current practitioners.

## Common Career Regrets

### 1. Not Learning Fundamentals Deeply

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│         Foundation Knowledge                           │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

Regret:
    │
    └─► Focused on frameworks, not fundamentals

Should Have Learned:
├─ Computer science fundamentals
├─ Algorithms and data structures
├─ System design principles
└─ How things actually work

Impact:
└─ Foundation would have helped everything
```

**The Lesson:**
- Frameworks come and go
- Fundamentals are timeless
- Deep understanding enables adaptation
- Invest in foundation early

## Summary

**Common regrets from 40-year career:**
1. **Not learning fundamentals** - Focused on frameworks
2. **Chasing technologies** - Wasted time on fads
3. **Not writing tests** - Short-term thinking
4. **Ignoring design** - Code over architecture
5. **Poor communication** - Technical skills only
6. **Risk avoidance** - Missed opportunities
7. **Not mentoring** - Missed growth opportunity
8. **Inadequate documentation** - Lost knowledge

**Key Lessons:**
- Invest in fundamentals early
- Balance speed and quality
- Develop communication skills
- Take calculated risks
- Think long-term

**Takeaway:** The regrets of experienced developers highlight the importance of fundamentals, quality practices, communication, and long-term thinking. Learn from these mistakes: invest in foundations, write tests, focus on design, communicate well, and take risks for growth.
