# Dr Nicole Forsgren's CONTROVERSIAL Take On Software Engineering vs. Craftsmanship

## Overview

Dr. Nicole Forsgren, co-author of "Accelerate" and DORA metrics creator, presents a controversial perspective on the balance between software engineering and craftsmanship. This summary explores her insights on measuring engineering effectiveness and the tension between craft and engineering.

```

│         The Tension                                    │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

Craftsmanship Focus:
    │
    ├─► Code quality
    ├─► Technical excellence
    ├─► Perfect code
    └─► Artisan approach

Engineering Focus:
    │
    ├─► Business value
    ├─► Delivery speed
    ├─► Measurable outcomes
    └─► Systematic approach
```

**The Controversy:**
- Craftsmanship emphasizes perfection
- Engineering emphasizes outcomes
- Balance is needed
- Measurement matters

## Summary

**The Controversial Take:**
- Engineering includes delivery, not just code

- Balance craft with delivery

**Key Points:**
1. **Delivery is engineering** - Not separate from code
2. **Measure outcomes** - DORA metrics matter
3. **Evidence-based** - Use data to improve
4. **Balance** - Quality AND delivery

**Takeaway:** Dr. Forsgren's perspective emphasizes that software engineering isn't complete without delivery capability. While craftsmanship matters, engineering effectiveness is measured by outcomes—delivery frequency, lead time, reliability, and recovery. The controversial part is prioritizing measurable outcomes over subjective craft quality, though both are needed.
