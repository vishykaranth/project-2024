## Intermittent Fasting Schedule

### Recommended Protocol: 16:8 (16-hour fast, 8-hour eating window)

**Eating Window:** 12:00 PM - 8:00 PM (or 11:00 AM - 7:00 PM based on preference)

**Fasting Window:** 8:00 PM - 12:00 PM next day

### Benefits of 16:8 for Vegans
- Enhanced autophagy
- Improved insulin sensitivity
- Better nutrient absorption during eating window
- Simpler meal planning
- Supports exercise recovery

### Breaking the Fast
- **First meal (12:00 PM):** Light, nutrient-dense meal
- **Main meal (2:00-3:00 PM):** Largest meal post-workout
- **Final meal (6:00-7:00 PM):** Moderate meal, 2-3 hours before sleep

### During Fasting Window
- ✅ Water (unlimited)
- ✅ Black coffee or green tea (no sugar/cream)
- ✅ Herbal teas
- ❌ No calories, no sweeteners

## Exercise Routine

### Weekly Exercise Schedule

#### Monday: Strength Training (Upper Body)
- **Time:** 1:00 PM (before main meal)
- **Duration:** 45-60 minutes
- **Exercises:**
  - Push-ups: 3 sets x 10-15
  - Pull-ups/Assisted pull-ups: 3 sets x 5-10
  - Dumbbell shoulder press: 3 sets x 10-12
  - Bent-over rows: 3 sets x 10-12
  - Bicep curls: 3 sets x 12-15
  - Tricep dips: 3 sets x 10-15
- **Post-workout:** Main meal within 1 hour

#### Tuesday: Cardio (HIIT)
- **Time:** 1:00 PM
- **Duration:** 30-40 minutes
- **Format:** 20 minutes HIIT + 10 minutes cool-down
- **Exercises:**
  - Jump squats: 30 sec on, 30 sec off (5 rounds)
  - Burpees: 30 sec on, 30 sec off (5 rounds)
  - Mountain climbers: 30 sec on, 30 sec off (5 rounds)
  - High knees: 30 sec on, 30 sec off (5 rounds)
  - Cool-down: Stretching and walking

#### Wednesday: Strength Training (Lower Body)
- **Time:** 1:00 PM
- **Duration:** 45-60 minutes
- **Exercises:**
  - Squats: 4 sets x 12-15
  - Lunges: 3 sets x 12 each leg
  - Deadlifts: 3 sets x 10-12
  - Leg press: 3 sets x 12-15
  - Calf raises: 3 sets x 15-20
  - Glute bridges: 3 sets x 15

#### Thursday: Active Recovery/Yoga
- **Time:** 1:00 PM
- **Duration:** 30-45 minutes
- **Activities:**
  - Yoga flow
  - Stretching
  - Light walking
  - Foam rolling

#### Friday: Full Body Strength
- **Time:** 1:00 PM
- **Duration:** 50-60 minutes
- **Exercises:**
  - Full body circuit (3 rounds):
    - Squat to press: 12 reps
    - Plank: 60 seconds
    - Deadlift: 10 reps
    - Push-ups: 12 reps
    - Lunges: 10 each leg
    - Rest 2 minutes between rounds

#### Saturday: Cardio (Steady State)
- **Time:** 10:00 AM (before eating window)
- **Duration:** 45-60 minutes
- **Activities:**
  - Running/jogging
  - Cycling
  - Swimming
  - Hiking
  - Moderate intensity, steady pace

#### Sunday: Rest or Light Activity
- **Time:** Flexible
- **Activities:**
  - Walking
  - Gentle stretching
  - Rest day
  - Light yoga

### Exercise Nutrition Timing

**Pre-Workout (if needed during fast):**
- Black coffee (30 minutes before)
- Water
- No calories needed during 16:8 fast

**Post-Workout (within 1 hour):**
- Main meal should include:
  - Complex carbs (quinoa, brown rice, sweet potato)
  - Plant protein (beans, lentils, tofu, tempeh)
  - Vegetables for micronutrients

**Hydration:**
- 2-3 liters water daily
- Electrolytes if intense/long workouts
- Coconut water post-workout (optional)

## Sleep Optimization

### Sleep Schedule
- **Target:** 7-9 hours per night
- **Bedtime:** 10:00 PM (adjust based on schedule)
- **Wake time:** 6:00 AM (allows for morning routine before eating window)

### Pre-Sleep Routine (9:00-10:00 PM)

**9:00 PM - Final Meal Complete**
- Last meal finished 2-3 hours before bed
- No heavy meals close to bedtime

**9:30 PM - Wind Down**
- Dim lights
- No screens (phone, TV, computer)
- Read a book or journal
- Meditation or gentle stretching

**10:00 PM - Sleep**
- Room temperature: 65-68°F (18-20°C)
- Dark room (blackout curtains)
- Quiet environment
- Comfortable bedding

### Sleep Hygiene Practices

**Daily Habits:**
- ✅ Consistent sleep/wake times (even weekends)
- ✅ Morning sunlight exposure (within 1 hour of waking)
- ✅ Limit caffeine after 2:00 PM
- ✅ No alcohol (disrupts sleep quality)
- ✅ Regular exercise (but not too close to bedtime)
- ✅ Cool, dark, quiet bedroom

**Avoid Before Bed:**
- ❌ Large meals (finish eating by 7:00 PM)
- ❌ Caffeine (after 2:00 PM)
- ❌ Screens (1 hour before bed)
- ❌ Intense exercise (finish by 6:00 PM)
- ❌ Stressful activities

### Sleep-Enhancing Foods (Earlier in Day)
- Magnesium-rich: Spinach, almonds, pumpkin seeds, black beans
- Tryptophan: Oats, bananas, seeds
- Complex carbs: Help with serotonin production
- Tart cherry juice: Natural melatonin source

## Nutritional Guidelines

### Daily Macronutrient Targets

**Calories:** 1,700-1,850 (adjust based on activity level and goals)

**Protein:** 65-75g daily
- Sources: Legumes, tofu, tempeh, seitan, quinoa, hemp seeds, nutritional yeast
- Distribute across meals
- Post-workout meal should be highest in protein

**Carbohydrates:** 250-300g daily
- Focus on complex carbs: Whole grains, sweet potatoes, fruits, vegetables
- Time around workouts for energy and recovery

**Fats:** 50-65g daily
- Healthy sources: Avocado, nuts, seeds, olive oil, coconut
- Essential for hormone production and nutrient absorption

**Fiber:** 55-65g daily
- Abundant in plant-based diet
- Supports gut health and satiety

### Key Micronutrients to Monitor

**Vitamin B12:** Supplement (2.4 mcg daily)
- Critical for vegans
- Not found in plant foods

**Vitamin D:** Supplement or sunlight (15-20 min daily)
- 600-800 IU daily
- Especially important in winter

**Iron:** 18mg daily (women), 8mg daily (men)
- Sources: Lentils, spinach, beans, fortified cereals
- Pair with vitamin C for absorption

**Calcium:** 1,000mg daily
- Sources: Fortified plant milks, tofu, leafy greens, tahini

**Omega-3:** Supplement or daily intake
- Sources: Flax seeds, chia seeds, walnuts, algae-based DHA/EPA

**Zinc:** 11mg daily (men), 8mg daily (women)
- Sources: Legumes, nuts, seeds, whole grains

### Meal Timing Principles

1. **Break fast with light meal** - Easy to digest, nutrient-dense
2. **Post-workout meal largest** - Recovery and muscle repair
3. **Evening meal moderate** - Finish 2-3 hours before sleep
4. **Hydration throughout day** - Especially during fast and workouts

## Meal Prep Guide

### Sunday Meal Prep (2-3 hours)

**1. Cook Grains (30 minutes)**
- 2 cups quinoa
- 2 cups brown rice
- Store in containers, lasts 5 days

**2. Prepare Proteins (45 minutes)**
- Bake tofu (marinated, 400°F for 25 min)
- Cook lentils (1 cup dry = 2.5 cups cooked)
- Prepare chickpeas (if from dry)
- Store in separate containers

**3. Roast Vegetables (30 minutes)**
- Sweet potatoes (cubed)
- Broccoli, cauliflower
- Bell peppers
- Store in containers

**4. Prepare Dressings/Sauces (15 minutes)**
- Tahini dressing (large batch)
- Lemon vinaigrette
- Store in jars

**5. Prep Smoothie Ingredients (15 minutes)**
- Portion frozen fruits into bags
- Pre-measure chia seeds, hemp seeds
- Store in freezer/containers

**6. Wash and Store Greens (10 minutes)**
- Wash all leafy greens
- Store in containers with paper towels
- Ready for quick salads

### Daily Prep (15-20 minutes)
- Assemble meals from prepped ingredients
- Fresh additions (sliced vegetables, herbs)
- Quick cooking (stir-fries, wraps)

### Time-Saving Tips
- Batch cook on weekends
- Use slow cooker for soups/curries
- Pre-portion snacks
- Keep emergency meals (frozen, canned)
- Prep smoothie ingredients night before

## Weekly Adjustments & Flexibility

### Adjusting for Goals

**Weight Loss:**
- Reduce calories by 200-300 daily
- Maintain protein intake
- Increase vegetables
- Extend fasting window if comfortable (18:6)

**Muscle Gain:**
- Increase calories by 200-300 daily
- Prioritize protein (80-90g daily)
- Post-workout meal critical
- Consider protein smoothie post-workout

**Maintenance:**
- Follow plan as outlined
- Monitor energy levels
- Adjust portions as needed

### Flexibility Guidelines

- **Swap meals** - Similar nutritional profiles
- **Adjust timing** - Shift eating window if needed
- **Modify portions** - Based on hunger and activity
- **Substitute ingredients** - Similar nutritional value
- **Rest days** - Lighter meals, more rest

### Listening to Your Body

- **Hunger cues** - Eat when hungry within eating window
- **Energy levels** - Adjust meal timing and composition
- **Recovery** - More rest if needed
- **Sleep quality** - Adjust evening meal timing
- **Performance** - Modify pre/post workout nutrition

## Troubleshooting

### Common Issues & Solutions

**Feeling Hungry During Fast:**
- Ensure adequate hydration
- Gradually extend fasting window
- Ensure sufficient calories in eating window
- Check meal timing and composition

**Low Energy:**
- Review calorie intake
- Check protein and carb distribution
- Ensure adequate sleep
- Consider B12 and iron levels

**Poor Sleep:**
- Finish eating earlier (by 6:00 PM)
- Reduce evening screen time
- Establish consistent routine
- Review caffeine intake

**Workout Performance:**
- Ensure post-workout meal is adequate
- Time workouts appropriately
- Stay hydrated
- Consider pre-workout coffee

**Digestive Issues:**
- Increase fiber gradually
- Ensure adequate hydration
- Consider probiotic foods
- Monitor food sensitivities
