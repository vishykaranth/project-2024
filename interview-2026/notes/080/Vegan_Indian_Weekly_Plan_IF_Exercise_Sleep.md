## Intermittent Fasting Schedule

### Recommended Protocol: 16:8 (16-hour fast, 8-hour eating window)

**Eating Window:** 12:00 PM - 8:00 PM (or 11:00 AM - 7:00 PM based on preference)

**Fasting Window:** 8:00 PM - 12:00 PM next day

### Breaking the Fast (Indian Style)
- **First meal (12:00 PM):** Light, nutrient-dense Indian breakfast
- **Main meal (2:00-3:00 PM):** Traditional Indian lunch post-workout
- **Final meal (6:00-7:00 PM):** Light Indian dinner, 2-3 hours before sleep

### During Fasting Window
- ✅ Water (unlimited)
- ✅ Black coffee or green tea (no sugar/milk)
- ✅ Herbal teas (ginger tea, tulsi tea)
- ❌ No calories, no sweeteners

## Exercise Routine

### Weekly Exercise Schedule

#### Monday: Strength Training (Upper Body)
- **Time:** 1:00 PM (before main meal)
- **Duration:** 45-60 minutes
- **Exercises:**
  - Push-ups: 3 sets x 10-15
  - Pull-ups/Assisted pull-ups: 3 sets x 5-10
  - Dumbbell shoulder press: 3 sets x 10-12
  - Bent-over rows: 3 sets x 10-12
  - Bicep curls: 3 sets x 12-15
  - Tricep dips: 3 sets x 10-15
- **Post-workout:** Main meal within 1 hour

#### Tuesday: Cardio (HIIT)
- **Time:** 1:00 PM
- **Duration:** 30-40 minutes
- **Format:** 20 minutes HIIT + 10 minutes cool-down
- **Exercises:**
  - Jump squats: 30 sec on, 30 sec off (5 rounds)
  - Burpees: 30 sec on, 30 sec off (5 rounds)
  - Mountain climbers: 30 sec on, 30 sec off (5 rounds)
  - High knees: 30 sec on, 30 sec off (5 rounds)
  - Cool-down: Stretching and walking

#### Wednesday: Strength Training (Lower Body)
- **Time:** 1:00 PM
- **Duration:** 45-60 minutes
- **Exercises:**
  - Squats: 4 sets x 12-15
  - Lunges: 3 sets x 12 each leg
  - Deadlifts: 3 sets x 10-12
  - Leg press: 3 sets x 12-15
  - Calf raises: 3 sets x 15-20
  - Glute bridges: 3 sets x 15

#### Thursday: Active Recovery/Yoga
- **Time:** 1:00 PM
- **Duration:** 30-45 minutes
- **Activities:**
  - Traditional yoga asanas
  - Surya Namaskar (Sun Salutation) - 5-10 rounds
  - Stretching
  - Light walking
  - Meditation

#### Friday: Full Body Strength
- **Time:** 1:00 PM
- **Duration:** 50-60 minutes
- **Exercises:**
  - Full body circuit (3 rounds):
    - Squat to press: 12 reps
    - Plank: 60 seconds
    - Deadlift: 10 reps
    - Push-ups: 12 reps
    - Lunges: 10 each leg
    - Rest 2 minutes between rounds

#### Saturday: Cardio (Steady State)
- **Time:** 10:00 AM (before eating window)
- **Duration:** 45-60 minutes
- **Activities:**
  - Running/jogging
  - Cycling
  - Swimming
  - Hiking
  - Moderate intensity, steady pace

#### Sunday: Rest or Light Activity
- **Time:** Flexible
- **Activities:**
  - Walking
  - Gentle yoga/stretching
  - Rest day
  - Light household activities

### Exercise Nutrition Timing

**Pre-Workout (if needed during fast):**
- Black coffee or green tea (30 minutes before)
- Water
- No calories needed during 16:8 fast

**Post-Workout (within 1 hour):**
- Main meal should include:
  - Complex carbs (brown rice, roti, dal)
  - Plant protein (dal, chana, rajma)

**Hydration:**
- 2-3 liters water daily

## Sleep Optimization

### Sleep Schedule
- **Target:** 7-9 hours per night
- **Bedtime:** 10:00 PM (adjust based on schedule)
- **Wake time:** 6:00 AM (allows for morning routine before eating window)

### Pre-Sleep Routine (9:00-10:00 PM)

**9:00 PM - Final Meal Complete**
- Last meal finished 2-3 hours before bed
- No heavy meals close to bedtime
- Light, easily digestible dinner

**9:30 PM - Wind Down**
- Dim lights
- No screens (phone, TV, computer)
- Read a book or journal
- Meditation or gentle stretching
- Herbal tea (chamomile, tulsi)

**10:00 PM - Sleep**
- Room temperature: 65-68°F (18-20°C)
- Dark room (blackout curtains)
- Quiet environment
- Comfortable bedding

### Sleep Hygiene Practices

**Daily Habits:**
- ✅ Consistent sleep/wake times (even weekends)
- ✅ Morning sunlight exposure (within 1 hour of waking)
- ✅ Limit caffeine after 2:00 PM
- ✅ No heavy meals close to bedtime
- ✅ Regular exercise (but not too close to bedtime)
- ✅ Cool, dark, quiet bedroom

**Avoid Before Bed:**
- ❌ Large meals (finish eating by 7:00 PM)
- ❌ Caffeine (after 2:00 PM)
- ❌ Screens (1 hour before bed)
- ❌ Intense exercise (finish by 6:00 PM)
- ❌ Stressful activities

### Sleep-Enhancing Indian Foods (Earlier in Day)
- **Magnesium-rich:** Spinach (palak), almonds (badam), pumpkin seeds, black beans (rajma)
- **Tryptophan:** Oats, bananas (kela), seeds
- **Complex carbs:** Brown rice, whole wheat roti - help with serotonin production
- **Warm milk alternative:** Warm almond/coconut milk with turmeric (golden milk) - earlier in evening

## Nutritional Guidelines

### Daily Macronutrient Targets

**Calories:** 1,700-1,850 (adjust based on activity level and goals)

**Protein:** 65-75g daily
- **Indian Sources:** Dal (toor, masoor, moong, urad), chana, rajma, chole, sambar, idli, dosa batter
- Distribute across meals
- Post-workout meal should be highest in protein

**Carbohydrates:** 250-300g daily
- **Indian Sources:** Brown rice, whole wheat roti, poha, upma, idli, dosa
- Focus on complex carbs
- Time around workouts for energy and recovery

**Fats:** 50-65g daily
- **Indian Sources:** Coconut oil, groundnut oil, mustard oil, ghee (vegan), nuts, seeds
- Essential for hormone production and nutrient absorption
- Use in moderation for cooking

**Fiber:** 55-65g daily
- Abundant in Indian plant-based diet
- Dal, vegetables, whole grains, fruits
- Supports gut health and satiety

### Key Micronutrients to Monitor

**Vitamin B12:** Supplement (2.4 mcg daily)
- Critical for vegans
- Not found in plant foods
- Especially important for Indian vegans

**Vitamin D:** Supplement or sunlight (15-20 min daily)
- 600-800 IU daily
- Morning sunlight exposure
- Especially important in winter

**Iron:** 18mg daily (women), 8mg daily (men)
- **Indian Sources:** Dal, spinach (palak), beans, jaggery, dates (khajur)
- Pair with vitamin C (lemon, amla) for absorption
- Avoid tea/coffee with iron-rich meals

**Calcium:** 1,000mg daily
- **Indian Sources:** Sesame seeds (til), ragi (finger millet), green leafy vegetables, fortified plant milks
- Include daily in diet

**Omega-3:** Supplement or daily intake
- **Indian Sources:** Flax seeds (alsi), chia seeds, walnuts (akhrot)
- Consider algae-based DHA/EPA supplement

**Zinc:** 11mg daily (men), 8mg daily (women)
- **Indian Sources:** Legumes, nuts, seeds, whole grains

### Meal Timing Principles

1. **Break fast with light meal** - Easy to digest, nutrient-dense Indian breakfast
2. **Post-workout meal largest** - Traditional Indian lunch for recovery
3. **Evening meal moderate** - Light Indian dinner, finish 2-3 hours before sleep
4. **Hydration throughout day** - Water, buttermilk, herbal teas

## Indian Meal Prep Guide

### Sunday Meal Prep (2-3 hours)

**1. Cook Dals (45 minutes)**
- Toor dal (2 cups dry = 5 cups cooked)
- Masoor dal (1 cup dry = 2.5 cups cooked)
- Store in containers, lasts 5 days
- Can prepare basic dal tadka base

**2. Prepare Legumes (60 minutes)**
- Soak and cook rajma (kidney beans)
- Soak and cook chana (chickpeas)
- Store in containers
- Can prepare chana masala base

**3. Prepare Rice & Grains (30 minutes)**
- Cook brown rice (2-3 cups)
- Prepare poha (flattened rice) - keep dry
- Store rice in containers

**4. Prepare Roti Dough (15 minutes)**
- Make whole wheat dough
- Store in airtight container
- Fresh rotis taste best, but dough can be prepped

**5. Prepare Chutneys (20 minutes)**
- Green chutney (coriander-mint) - large batch
- Coconut chutney - store in fridge
- Tomato chutney
- Store in jars

**6. Prepare Spice Mixes (10 minutes)**
- Garam masala (if making fresh)
- Sambar powder (if making)
- Store in airtight containers

**7. Wash and Store Vegetables (15 minutes)**
- Wash all vegetables
- Chop and store in containers
- Keep leafy greens fresh with paper towels

**8. Prepare Dosa/Idli Batter (if making) (30 minutes)**
- Soak rice and urad dal
- Grind and ferment (overnight)
- Store in fridge

### Daily Prep (20-30 minutes)
- Fresh rotis (best made fresh)
- Fresh sabzis (vegetable curries)
- Assemble meals from prepped ingredients
- Fresh additions (herbs, lemon, onions)

### Time-Saving Tips
- Batch cook dals on weekends
- Use pressure cooker for faster cooking
- Pre-soak legumes overnight
- Keep pre-made spice mixes ready
- Prepare chutneys in bulk
- Use frozen vegetables when fresh not available
- Keep emergency meals (canned dal, ready-to-eat)

### Indian Cooking Techniques
- **Tadka (Tempering):** Quick flavor boost for dals
- **Pressure Cooking:** Faster dal and legume cooking
- **Roasting:** For better spice flavors
- **Steaming:** For idli, dhokla (healthier)
- **Sautéing:** For vegetables

## Troubleshooting

### Common Issues & Solutions

**Feeling Hungry During Fast:**
- Ensure adequate hydration
- Gradually extend fasting window
- Ensure sufficient calories in eating window
- Check meal timing and composition
- Include more fiber (dal, vegetables)

**Low Energy:**
- Review calorie intake
- Check protein and carb distribution
- Ensure adequate sleep
- Consider B12 and iron levels
- Include iron-rich foods with vitamin C

**Poor Sleep:**
- Finish eating earlier (by 7:00 PM)
- Reduce evening screen time
- Establish consistent routine
- Review caffeine intake
- Light dinner, avoid heavy spices late

**Workout Performance:**
- Ensure post-workout meal is adequate
- Time workouts appropriately
- Stay hydrated
- Consider pre-workout coffee/tea
- Include complex carbs post-workout

**Digestive Issues:**
- Increase fiber gradually
- Ensure adequate hydration
- Use digestive aids (jeera, hing, ginger)
- Monitor spice tolerance
- Include buttermilk (chaas)
- Soak legumes properly before cooking

**Spice Tolerance:**
