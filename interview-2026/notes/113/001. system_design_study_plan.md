# 🏗️ Principal Engineer System Design Study Plan

**12-Week Comprehensive Program**

---

## 📊 Overview

- **Total Topics:** 48
- **Estimated Time:** 240-280 hours
- **Duration:** 12 weeks
- **Weekly Commitment:** 20-25 hours

---

## 📚 Weeks 1-2: Architectural Foundations & Scale

### Distributed Systems Fundamentals
**Time Estimate:** 6-8 hours

- CAP theorem deep dive and practical implications
- Consistency models: strong, eventual, causal
- Consensus algorithms: Paxos, Raft, Byzantine fault tolerance
- Time and ordering in distributed systems (Lamport clocks, vector clocks)

### Scalability Patterns & Techniques
**Time Estimate:** 5-7 hours

- Horizontal vs vertical scaling trade-offs
- Sharding strategies: hash-based, range-based, geography-based
- Load balancing algorithms and consistent hashing
- Replication strategies and eventual consistency patterns

### Data Storage Architecture
**Time Estimate:** 6-8 hours

- SQL vs NoSQL: when to use each
- Database internals: B-trees, LSM trees, indexes
- Data modeling for scale: denormalization, CQRS
- Data warehousing vs data lakes vs lakehouses

### Practical Exercise: Design Twitter/X at Scale
**Time Estimate:** 4-5 hours

Design end-to-end architecture covering: tweet ingestion, timeline generation, search, notifications, and analytics

---

## ⚡ Weeks 3-4: Performance Engineering & Reliability

### Caching Strategies
**Time Estimate:** 5-6 hours

- Cache hierarchies: L1/L2/CDN/application/database
- Eviction policies: LRU, LFU, ARC
- Cache invalidation strategies and consistency
- Write-through vs write-back vs write-around

### High Availability & Fault Tolerance
**Time Estimate:** 6-8 hours

- SLA/SLO/SLI design and error budgets
- Circuit breakers, bulkheads, and retry patterns
- Health checks and graceful degradation
- Multi-region active-active architectures

### Performance Optimization
**Time Estimate:** 5-7 hours

- Profiling and bottleneck identification
- Database query optimization and indexing strategies
- API design for performance: pagination, filtering, batch operations
- Asynchronous processing and message queues

### Practical Exercise: Design Netflix Video Streaming
**Time Estimate:** 4-5 hours

Focus on CDN strategy, adaptive bitrate streaming, regional failover, and handling millions of concurrent streams

---

## 📊 Weeks 5-6: Data Processing & Analytics

### Stream Processing Architecture
**Time Estimate:** 6-7 hours

- Event-driven architectures and event sourcing
- Kafka, Kinesis, Pub/Sub comparison
- Stream processing frameworks: Flink, Spark Streaming
- Exactly-once semantics and idempotency

### Batch Processing & ETL
**Time Estimate:** 5-6 hours

- MapReduce paradigm and modern alternatives
- Data pipeline orchestration: Airflow, Prefect
- Delta Lake, Iceberg for ACID transactions
- Incremental processing and change data capture

### Real-time Analytics
**Time Estimate:** 5-6 hours

- OLAP vs OLTP database design
- Columnar databases: Clickhouse, Druid
- Time-series databases and aggregation strategies
- Approximate algorithms: HyperLogLog, Count-Min Sketch

### Practical Exercise: Design Uber's Real-time Pricing & Analytics
**Time Estimate:** 4-5 hours

Design surge pricing system, driver-rider matching, real-time dashboards for millions of concurrent users

---

## 🔌 Weeks 7-8: Microservices & API Architecture

### Microservices Patterns
**Time Estimate:** 6-7 hours

- Service decomposition strategies and domain-driven design
- Inter-service communication: REST, gRPC, GraphQL
- Service mesh architecture: Istio, Linkerd
- Saga pattern for distributed transactions

### API Design & Versioning
**Time Estimate:** 5-6 hours

- RESTful API best practices and HATEOAS
- GraphQL schema design and N+1 problem
- API versioning strategies and backward compatibility
- Rate limiting, throttling, and quota management

### Service Discovery & Configuration
**Time Estimate:** 4-5 hours

- Service registry patterns: Consul, Eureka
- Client-side vs server-side discovery
- Distributed configuration management
- Feature flags and canary deployments

### Practical Exercise: Design Shopify/Amazon Checkout System
**Time Estimate:** 5-6 hours

Multi-service architecture for inventory, payments, order processing, notifications with strong consistency requirements

---

## 🔒 Weeks 9-10: Security & Observability

### Security Architecture
**Time Estimate:** 6-8 hours

- Authentication: OAuth 2.0, OIDC, JWT best practices
- Authorization: RBAC, ABAC, policy engines
- Encryption at rest and in transit
- DDoS mitigation and WAF strategies
- Zero-trust architecture principles

### Observability & Monitoring
**Time Estimate:** 6-7 hours

- Metrics, logs, and traces (three pillars)
- Distributed tracing: Jaeger, Zipkin
- Log aggregation and analysis
- SLI/SLO-based alerting strategies
- Chaos engineering principles

### Incident Management
**Time Estimate:** 4-5 hours

- On-call rotations and runbook design
- Incident response and post-mortem culture
- Disaster recovery and backup strategies
- Capacity planning and forecasting

### Practical Exercise: Design a Banking Platform
**Time Estimate:** 5-6 hours

Focus on security, compliance, audit trails, fraud detection, and financial transaction guarantees

## 📝 Study Tips

### Weekly Routine
1. **Mon-Tue:** Deep dive into 1-2 theoretical topics (6-8 hours)
2. **Wed-Thu:** Continue learning + hands-on experiments (6-8 hours)
3. **Fri:** Practical exercise or case study (4-5 hours)
4. **Sat-Sun:** Review, mock interview practice, or catch up (4-6 hours)

### Best Practices
- **Take notes:** Document key insights and architectural patterns
- **Draw diagrams:** Practice whiteboarding system designs
- **Ask "why":** Understand trade-offs, not just solutions
- **Stay current:** Read recent tech blogs from major companies
- **Practice explaining:** Teach concepts to solidify understanding
- **Mock interviews:** Schedule at least 2-3 mock sessions in final weeks

✅ Design systems that handle millions/billions of users  
✅ Make informed trade-off decisions with clear justifications  
✅ Identify bottlenecks and propose optimization strategies  
✅ Communicate complex architectures clearly and confidently

✅ Lead architectural discussions with senior stakeholders  
✅ Evaluate and select appropriate technologies for use cases  
✅ Design for failure and build resilient systems
