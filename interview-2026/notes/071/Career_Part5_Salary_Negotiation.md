# 10 Rules of Salary Negotiation

## Overview

This guide covers 10 essential rules for salary negotiation, specifically for tech roles at companies like Meta, Google, and other FAANG companies. These strategies can help you raise your offer significantly.

## The 10 Rules

### Rule 1: Never Accept the First Offer

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│         First Offer Strategy                           │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

Why:
├─► Companies expect negotiation
├─► First offer is usually lower than budget
├─► Room for 10-20% increase typically
└─► Shows confidence and value

Response:
"Thank you for the offer. I'm very excited about this
opportunity. Based on my research and experience, I was
hoping for something in the range of [X-Y]. Is there
flexibility in the compensation package?"
```

### Rule 2: Do Your Research

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│         Research Sources                               │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

Salary Data:
├─► Levels.fyi (best for tech)
├─► Glassdoor

Factors to Research:

├─► Total compensation (not just base)
└─► Stock/equity value
```

### Rule 3: Negotiate Total Compensation

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│         Total Compensation Components                  │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

Components:
├─► Base Salary
├─► Sign-on Bonus
├─► Stock/Equity (RSUs)
├─► Annual Bonus
├─► Benefits (401k match, etc.)
└─► Relocation (if applicable)

Strategy:
├─► Focus on total package
├─► Be flexible on components
├─► Consider long-term value
└─► Negotiate each component
```

### Rule 4: Use Competing Offers

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│         Competing Offers Strategy                      │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

How to Use:
"I have another offer from [Company] for [amount].
I'm very interested in this role, but the compensation
is an important factor. Is there any flexibility?"

Benefits:
├─► Shows market value
├─► Creates urgency
├─► Demonstrates demand
└─► Often leads to better offer

Timing:
├─► Mention after initial offer
├─► Be respectful
└─► Don't make threats
```

## Negotiation Scripts

### Initial Response to Offer

```
"Thank you so much for the offer. I'm really excited about
this opportunity and the team. I've done some research on
compensation for this role and level, and I was hoping we
could discuss the package. Based on my experience and the
market, I was thinking something in the range of [X-Y] for
total compensation. Is there flexibility there?"
```

### Counter-Offer Response

```
"I really appreciate you working with me on this. I'm
very interested in joining the team. The [specific amount]
you mentioned is closer, but I was hoping we could get to
[specific amount] given [reason: experience, competing
offer, market research]. Would that be possible?"
```

### Final Acceptance

```
"Thank you for working with me on the compensation. I'm
excited to accept the offer and join the team. I'm looking
forward to [specific thing about role/company]."
```

## Common Scenarios

### Scenario 1: Low Initial Offer

```
Response:
"I'm very excited about this opportunity. However, based
on my research for this role and level at similar companies,
I was expecting something in the range of [X-Y]. Given my
experience with [relevant skills], I believe I can bring
significant value. Is there flexibility in the compensation
package?"
```

### Scenario 2: They Say "This is Our Best Offer"

```
Response:
"I understand, and I appreciate you working with me on
this. I'm very interested in this role. Is there any
flexibility in other areas like sign-on bonus, stock,
or start date that might help bridge the gap?"
```

### Scenario 3: Competing Offer

```
Response:
"I have another offer from [Company] for [amount] total
compensation. I'm very interested in this role because
[reason], but compensation is an important factor. Is
there any way we could get closer to that number?"
```

## Key Takeaways

### Essential Rules:

1. ✅ **Never accept first offer** - Always negotiate
2. ✅ **Do your research** - Know market rates
3. ✅ **Negotiate total comp** - Not just salary
4. ✅ **Use competing offers** - Show market value
5. ✅ **Be professional** - Positive and collaborative
6. ✅ **Know your walk-away** - Set boundaries
7. ✅ **Get it in writing** - Document everything
8. ✅ **Consider long-term** - Total value matters
9. ✅ **Practice** - Build confidence
10. ✅ **Be flexible** - Multiple components to negotiate

### Expected Outcomes:

- **10-20% increase** typical for first negotiation
- **20-30% increase** possible with competing offers
- **Higher sign-on bonus** often easier than base salary
- **Stock adjustments** common in tech companies

## Summary

**Key Success Factors:**
1. Research market rates thoroughly
2. Never accept first offer
3. Negotiate total compensation
4. Be professional and positive
5. Practice your approach
6. Consider long-term value
7. Get everything in writing

**Remember**: Negotiation is expected and normal. Companies budget for it. Being prepared and professional can significantly increase your compensation package!
