# 🚫 Principal Anti-Patterns (Mapped to the 200)

---

## 🧠 Mindset & Ownership Anti-Patterns

### 1. **Hero Fixing**

> “One engineer knows how this works.”

**Violates:**

* Automation beats memory
* Systems outlive people
* Remove tribal knowledge

**Principal Response:**

> Encode fixes into tooling, defaults, and runbooks.

---

### 2. **Blame-Driven Postmortems**

> “Who caused this?”

**Violates:**

* Incidents are system failures
* Blameless ≠ accountability-free

**Principal Response:**

> Focus on *why the system allowed the mistake*.

---

### 3. **Documentation as the Fix**

> “We wrote a doc so it won’t happen again.”

**Violates:**

* Documentation is not a control
* Automation > memory

**Principal Response:**

> If the system didn’t change, nothing changed.

---

### 4. **Optimizing for Cleverness**

> “This is elegant.”

**Violates:**

* Favor boring solutions
* Consistency beats cleverness

**Principal Response:**

> Optimize for 3am on-call, not code aesthetics.

---

### 5. **One-Off Thinking**

> “This was a unique incident.”

**Violates:**

* Failures repeat
* Learning must compound

**Principal Response:**

> Assume recurrence until proven otherwise.

---

## 🧱 Architecture Anti-Patterns

### 6. **Shared Everything**

> “We’ll just reuse the same thread pool / DB / cache.”

**Violates:**

* Isolation > optimization
* Shared resources create coupling

**Failure Mode:**
Cascading outages.

---

### 7. **Retry as Reliability**

> “Just add retries.”

**Violates:**

* Retry ≠ recovery
* Retries amplify failures

**Principal Response:**

> Add backoff, jitter, and circuit breakers — or don’t retry.

---

### 8. **No Backpressure**

> “Kafka will buffer it.”

**Violates:**

* Every queue is a risk
* Backpressure is mandatory

**Failure Mode:**
Lag explosions, OOMs, silent data loss.

---

### 9. **Exactly-Once Fantasy**

> “Kafka guarantees exactly-once.”

**Violates:**

* Exactly-once is contextual
* Idempotency beats coordination

**Principal Response:**

> Design *effectively-once* systems.

---

### 10. **Ignoring Latency Compounding**

> “Each call is fast.”

**Violates:**

* Latency compounds
* Slow dependencies dominate

**Failure Mode:**
P99 collapses under scale.

---

## ☕ Spring Boot Anti-Patterns

### 11. **Annotation-Driven Thinking**

> “Spring will handle it.”

**Violates:**

* @Transactional is not magic
* Auto-config hides behavior

**Failure Mode:**
Silent data corruption.

---

### 12. **Blocking Everywhere**

> “It works locally.”

**Violates:**

* Blocking starves threads
* MVC is not scalable by default

**Failure Mode:**
Thread pool exhaustion.

---

### 13. **God Application Context**

> “We load everything.”

**Violates:**

* Context size impacts startup
* Bean count affects memory

**Failure Mode:**
Slow deploys, OOMs, cold start pain.

---

### 14. **Health Checks That Kill You**

> “If dependency is down, pod is unhealthy.”

**Violates:**

* Liveness ≠ readiness

**Failure Mode:**
Restart storms during outages.

---

### 15. **Logging as Debugging Tool**

> “Add more logs.”

**Violates:**

* Logging is a performance decision

**Failure Mode:**
Latency spikes, disk exhaustion.

---

## 🧵 Kafka Anti-Patterns

### 16. **Scaling Consumers Instead of Keys**

> “Add more consumers.”

**Violates:**

* Partitions are capacity units
* Keys define scalability

**Failure Mode:**
Hot partitions that never heal.

---

### 17. **Auto-Commit in Production**

> “Kafka handles offsets.”

**Violates:**

* Offset commit ≠ processing

**Failure Mode:**
Silent data loss.

---

### 18. **Single Topic for Everything**

> “One topic is simpler.”

**Violates:**

* Isolation > optimization

**Failure Mode:**
Mixed SLAs, impossible tuning.

---

### 19. **Replays Without Safeguards**

> “We’ll just replay the topic.”

**Violates:**

* Replays are outages in disguise

**Failure Mode:**
Duplicate writes, downstream meltdowns.

---

### 20. **DLQ Without Ownership**

> “We have a DLQ.”

**Violates:**

* Failures must be actionable

**Failure Mode:**
Infinite data graveyards.

---

## 🗄️ Database Anti-Patterns

### 21. **Oversized Connection Pools**

> “More connections = faster.”

**Violates:**

* DBs fail by saturation

**Failure Mode:**
DB collapse under load.

---

### 22. **Long Transactions**

> “It’s safer.”

**Violates:**

* Long transactions block progress

**Failure Mode:**
Deadlocks, lock contention.

---

### 23. **ORM Blindness**

> “Hibernate handles queries.”

**Violates:**

* ORM hides query cost

**Failure Mode:**
N+1 surprises in prod.

---

### 24. **Schema Changes at Deploy Time**

> “Flyway runs on startup.”

**Violates:**

* Schema changes are the hardest deploy

**Failure Mode:**
Deploy-time outages.

---

### 25. **OFFSET Pagination**

> “Page 1000.”

**Violates:**

* OFFSET pagination breaks at scale

**Failure Mode:**
Latency spikes and timeouts.

---

## 🚨 Incident Response Anti-Patterns

### 26. **Fixing Root Cause During Incident**

> “Let’s refactor now.”

**Violates:**

* Stop the bleeding first

**Failure Mode:**
Worse outage.

---

### 27. **Touching the DB First**

> “Let’s tune queries.”

**Violates:**

* Don’t tune DB under load

**Failure Mode:**
Irreversible damage.

---

### 28. **All-Hands Panic**

> “Everyone jump in.”

**Violates:**

* Design for humans under stress

**Failure Mode:**
Conflicting changes, no ownership.

---

### 29. **Alert Storms**

> “Alert on everything.”

**Violates:**

* Alerts must be actionable

**Failure Mode:**
Real incidents ignored.

---

### 30. **No Runbooks**

> “We’ll figure it out.”

**Violates:**

* Reduce cognitive load

**Failure Mode:**
Slow MTTR, burnout.
