---

# 🧱 THE PRINCIPAL WHITEBOARD FLOW

### *(Failure-first, boundary-driven design)*

Think of this as **what you draw + what you say**.

---

## STEP 1 — DRAW THE CORE GOAL (1 box)

### Whiteboard

* Draw **one central box**
* Label it with the *single most critical outcome*

Example:

```
[ Place Order ]
```

### Say

> “I’ll start by identifying the one thing this system must never get wrong.”

🔑 **Kill shot embedded**

> *“If everything is critical, nothing is.”*

**Why this works:**
You’ve framed the design around **invariants**, not features.

---

## STEP 2 — DEFINE NON-NEGOTIABLE INVARIANTS

### Whiteboard

Inside or under the core box, list 2–3 bullets:

* Payment captured once
* Inventory not oversold
* Order state consistent

### Say

> “These are invariants. Everything else is negotiable.”

🔑 **Kill shot embedded**

> *“Architecture is deciding what you refuse to make unreliable.”*

**Why this works:**
You’ve set a **reliability budget**.

---

## STEP 3 — DRAW FAILURE ZONES (BOUNDARIES)

### Whiteboard

Surround the core with outer boxes:

```
[ Place Order ]
     |
-------------------------
| Inventory | Payment |
-------------------------
| Email | Analytics |
-------------------------
```

Use **distance** to signal importance.

### Say

> “I place failure-prone components farther from the invariant core.”

🔑 **Kill shot embedded**

> *“Boundaries exist to contain damage, not to organize code.”*

**Why this works:**
This visually communicates **blast radius control**.

---

## STEP 4 — DECIDE SYNCHRONOUS VS ASYNCHRONOUS PATHS

### Whiteboard

* Solid arrows → synchronous
* Dotted arrows → asynchronous

Example:

```
Order → Payment (sync)
Order → Email (async)
```

### Say

> “Every synchronous call is a bet on availability, so I use them sparingly.”

🔑 **Kill shot embedded**

> *“Every synchronous call tightens coupling.”*

**Why this works:**
You’re explicitly trading **simplicity vs availability**.

---

## STEP 5 — MARK WHERE FAILURE IS ACCEPTABLE

### Whiteboard

Put ❌ next to:

* Notifications
* Metrics
* Recommendations

### Say

> “These paths are allowed to fail without affecting the core.”

🔑 **Kill shot embedded**

> *“A reliable system knows when to say no.”*

**Why this works:**
You’re designing **graceful degradation** explicitly.

---

## STEP 6 — DEFINE DATA OWNERSHIP & CONSISTENCY

### Whiteboard

Draw separate data stores per domain:

```
Order DB
Inventory DB
Payment Store
```

Label:

* Strong consistency
* Eventual consistency

### Say

> “Strong consistency is expensive, so I reserve it for invariants.”

🔑 **Kill shot embedded**

> *“Strong consistency becomes a global mutex at scale.”*

**Why this works:**
You’re showing **selective correctness**, not dogma.

---

## STEP 7 — DESIGN FOR BACKPRESSURE

### Whiteboard

Add:

* Queue between producer/consumer
* Note “buffer” or “pressure relief”

### Say

> “Queues buy me time, not correctness.”

🔑 **Kill shot embedded**

> *“Backpressure is a design feature, not an implementation detail.”*

**Why this works:**
You’ve acknowledged **burst traffic and downstream slowness**.

---

## STEP 8 — IDENTIFY HOT SPOTS & CONTENTION

### Whiteboard

Circle potential hot keys:

* Inventory item
* User account
* Order ID

### Say

> “If optimistic locking thrashes here, the data model is wrong.”

🔑 **Kill shot embedded**

> *“Hot keys are architectural bugs.”*

**Why this works:**
You’re proactively redesigning contention, not reacting.

---

## STEP 9 — DEFINE DEGRADATION STRATEGY

### Whiteboard

Add a side note:

```
If load > X:
- Disable recommendations
- Delay emails
- Shed traffic
```

### Say

> “The goal isn’t uptime — it’s controlled degradation.”

🔑 **Kill shot embedded**

> *“Good architecture fails in layers.”*

**Why this works:**
You’ve shown **operational realism**.

---

## STEP 10 — CALL OUT WHAT YOU ARE NOT SOLVING

### Whiteboard

Create a small box:

```
Out of Scope (by design):
- Real-time analytics
- Perfect ordering
- Cross-region sync
```

### Say

> “I’m explicitly not solving these in the first version.”

🔑 **Kill shot embedded**

> *“The hardest part of architecture is choosing what not to solve.”*

**Why this works:**
Principals **de-scope confidently**.

---

## STEP 11 — FINAL CLOSE (STOP DRAWING)

Put the marker down.

### Say

> “This design prioritizes invariants, isolates failure, and degrades intentionally.”

🔑 **Final kill shot**

> *“Architecture is deciding what breaks first — and making sure it’s not the user.”*
