## ❌ DISAGREEMENT #1 — *“Why are you using JPA at all?”*

> **Interviewer:**
> “Honestly, I don’t like this design.
> At 100k TPS, JPA is too slow and opaque.
> Why not just use raw SQL or DynamoDB and skip PoEAA entirely?”

⏸️ **Pause. Don’t panic.**

### What I’m testing

* Do you understand **where JPA breaks**
* Can you separate **concepts from tools**
* Do you know when to **abandon elegance for physics**
---

"At 100k TPS, I would move away from **Spring Data JPA** and **Hibernate** for the core transaction path. I would use **Spring Data JDBC** or **JdbcTemplate** for relational needs, or move entirely to a **Key-Value Store like DynamoDB** for sub-millisecond, predictable scaling. I'd keep the **Service Layer** pattern to organize logic, but I'd treat the data layer as a **Transaction Script** rather than a complex **Domain Model** to avoid the hydration overhead."
"Synchronous is simpler to code, but it couples my **Availability** to my **Downstream Dependencies**. At 100k TPS, I use messaging to provide **Pressure Relief** and **Temporal Decoupling**, ensuring that a slow bank or a DB spike doesn't take down the entire storefront."

## ❌ DISAGREEMENT #2 — *“Your Domain Model is overkill”*

> **Interviewer (interrupts):**
> “This is just placing an order.
> Why do you need a Domain Model at all?
> Transaction Script is faster and simpler.”

⚠️ This is a trap.
They want to see if you’re **dogmatic** or **pragmatic**.

---

3. When the Script "Hits the Wall"
The danger of Transaction Script isn't performance (it’s faster); it’s entropy. As the business asks for more features, your simple script becomes a "God Method."

Duplicate Logic: If CancelOrder also needs to check inventory, do you copy-paste the SQL?

State Explosion: If an order can be "Pending," "Fraud-Checked," "Partially-Paid," or "Backordered," the if/else chains in your script will eventually lead to bugs that only a Domain Model's State Pattern could solve cleanly.

4. The 100k TPS Verdict
For a "Place Order" flow at massive scale:

Ditch JPA Entities: They are too slow for the "Hot Path."

Use Transaction Script: Implement the logic procedurally using JdbcTemplate or JDBI.

Use Data Transfer Objects (DTOs): Keep your data structures "dumb" and your logic concentrated in the service.

Embrace Row Data Gateway: If you need some organization, create simple objects that map 1:1 to rows without the "Rich Behavior."
